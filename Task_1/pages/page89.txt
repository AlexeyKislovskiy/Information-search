<p><b>Lower Sorbian</b> (<a href="/wiki/Endonym" class="mw-redirect" title="Endonym">endonym</a>: <i lang="dsb">dolnoserbšćina</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/West_Slavic_languages" title="West Slavic languages">West Slavic</a> <a href="/wiki/Minority_language" title="Minority language">minority language</a> spoken in eastern <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> in the historical province of <a href="/wiki/Lower_Lusatia" title="Lower Lusatia">Lower Lusatia</a>, today part of <a href="/wiki/Brandenburg" title="Brandenburg">Brandenburg</a>.
</p><p>Standard Lower Sorbian is one of the two literary <a href="/wiki/Sorbian_languages" title="Sorbian languages">Sorbian languages</a>, the other being the more widely spoken <span class="clarify-content" style="padding-left:0.1em; padding-right:0.1em; color:#595959; border:1px solid #DDD;">standard</span><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Clarify" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="This passage needs to be better explained. (September 2021)">clarify</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> <a href="/wiki/Upper_Sorbian" class="mw-redirect" title="Upper Sorbian">Upper Sorbian</a>. The Lower Sorbian literary standard was developed in the 18th century, based on a southern form of the Cottbus dialect.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Standard_language" title="Standard language">standard variety</a> of Lower Sorbian has received structural influence from Upper Sorbian.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Lower Sorbian is spoken in and around the city of <a href="/wiki/Cottbus" title="Cottbus">Cottbus</a> in <a href="/wiki/Brandenburg" title="Brandenburg">Brandenburg</a>. Signs in this region are typically bilingual, and Cottbus has a <i><a href="/wiki/Lower_Sorbian_Gymnasium_Cottbus" title="Lower Sorbian Gymnasium Cottbus">Lower Sorbian Gymnasium</a></i> where one language of instruction is Lower Sorbian. It is a heavily <a href="/wiki/Endangered_language" title="Endangered language">endangered language</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Most native speakers today belong to the older generations.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Phonology">Phonology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lower_Sorbian_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Phonology"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Bilinguale_Stra%C3%9Fenbeschilderung_Cottbus.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Bilinguale_Stra%C3%9Fenbeschilderung_Cottbus.jpg/220px-Bilinguale_Stra%C3%9Fenbeschilderung_Cottbus.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Bilinguale_Stra%C3%9Fenbeschilderung_Cottbus.jpg/330px-Bilinguale_Stra%C3%9Fenbeschilderung_Cottbus.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Bilinguale_Stra%C3%9Fenbeschilderung_Cottbus.jpg/440px-Bilinguale_Stra%C3%9Fenbeschilderung_Cottbus.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3052" data-file-height="2289" /></a><figcaption>Bilingual road sign in <a href="/wiki/Cottbus" title="Cottbus">Cottbus</a>, Germany</figcaption></figure>
<p>The phonology of Lower Sorbian has been greatly influenced by <a href="/wiki/Language_contact" title="Language contact">contact</a> with <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>, especially in Cottbus and larger towns. For example, German-influenced pronunciation tends to have a <a href="/wiki/Voiced_uvular_fricative" title="Voiced uvular fricative">voiced uvular fricative</a> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[ʁ]</span> instead of the alveolar trill <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[r]</span>. In villages and rural areas, German influence is less marked, and the pronunciation is more "typically Slavic".
</p>

<ul><li><span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/m,<span class="wrap"> </span>mʲ,<span class="wrap"> </span>p,<span class="wrap"> </span>pʲ,<span class="wrap"> </span>b,<span class="wrap"> </span>bʲ/</span> are <a href="/wiki/Bilabial_consonant" title="Bilabial consonant">bilabial</a>, whereas <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/f,<span class="wrap"> </span>v/</span> are <a href="/wiki/Labiodental_consonant" title="Labiodental consonant">labiodental</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEStone2002605_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStone2002605-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/w,<span class="wrap"> </span>wʲ/</span> are <a href="/wiki/Labialized_velar_consonant" title="Labialized velar consonant">labiovelar</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> although the latter may be a <a href="/wiki/Labial%E2%80%93palatal_approximant" class="mw-redirect" title="Labial–palatal approximant">labial–palatal approximant</a>.</li>
<li>Consonants in parentheses are allophones of another consonant before another consonant or vowel, for example <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/m/</span> may <a href="/wiki/Palatalization_(sound_change)" title="Palatalization (sound change)">palatalize</a> to <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/mʲ/</span> before front vowels or <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/j/</span>, and <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/n/</span> may <a href="/wiki/Assimilation_(phonology)" title="Assimilation (phonology)">assimilate</a> to <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/ŋ/</span> before <a href="/wiki/Velar_consonants" class="mw-redirect" title="Velar consonants">velar consonants</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Proto-Slavic" class="mw-redirect" title="Proto-Slavic">Proto-Slavic</a> contrasts between <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/m,<span class="wrap"> </span>p,<span class="wrap"> </span>b,<span class="wrap"> </span>v/</span> and their palatalized counterparts has been lost phonetically in Lower Sorbian, with the marginal phonemes occurring only before certain vowels. The contrasts between <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/t,<span class="wrap"> </span>d/</span> and their palatalized counterparts has evolved into a contrast between <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/t,<span class="wrap"> </span>d/</span> and <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/ɕ,<span class="wrap"> </span>ʑ/</span>. The contrast between <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/l/</span> and its palatalized counterpart has evolved into a contrast between <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/w,<span class="wrap"> </span>l/</span> while the contrasts between <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/n,<span class="wrap"> </span>r/</span> and their palatalized counterparts has remained intact and the contrasts between <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/s,<span class="wrap"> </span>z/</span> and their palatalized counterparts no longer exists.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/n,<span class="wrap"> </span>nʲ,<span class="wrap"> </span>l,<span class="wrap"> </span>r,<span class="wrap"> </span>rʲ/</span> are alveolar <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[<a href="/wiki/Voiced_alveolar_nasal" class="mw-redirect" title="Voiced alveolar nasal">n</a>,<span class="wrap"> </span>nʲ,<span class="wrap"> </span><a href="/wiki/Voiced_alveolar_lateral_approximant" class="mw-redirect" title="Voiced alveolar lateral approximant">l</a>,<span class="wrap"> </span><a href="/wiki/Voiced_alveolar_trill" class="mw-redirect" title="Voiced alveolar trill">r</a>,<span class="wrap"> </span>rʲ]</span>, whereas <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/t,<span class="wrap"> </span>d,<span class="wrap"> </span>t͡s,<span class="wrap"> </span>s,<span class="wrap"> </span>z/</span> are dental <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[<a href="/wiki/Voiceless_dental_plosive" class="mw-redirect" title="Voiceless dental plosive">t̪</a>,<span class="wrap"> </span><a href="/wiki/Voiced_dental_plosive" class="mw-redirect" title="Voiced dental plosive">d̪</a>,<span class="wrap"> </span><a href="/wiki/Voiceless_dental_affricate" class="mw-redirect" title="Voiceless dental affricate">t̪͡s̪</a>,<span class="wrap"> </span><a href="/wiki/Voiceless_dental_sibilant_fricative" class="mw-redirect" title="Voiceless dental sibilant fricative">s̪</a>,<span class="wrap"> </span><a href="/wiki/Voiced_dental_sibilant_fricative" class="mw-redirect" title="Voiced dental sibilant fricative">z̪</a>]</span>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEStone2002605_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStone2002605-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/t͡ʃ,<span class="wrap"> </span>ʃ,<span class="wrap"> </span>ʒ/</span> have been variously transcribed with <span class="nowrap">&#x27e8;<span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA" lang="und-Latn-fonipa">t͡ʃ, ʃ, ʒ</span>&#x27e9;</span><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHannusch1998_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHannusch1998-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEStone2002_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStone2002-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and <span class="nowrap">&#x27e8;<span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA" lang="und-Latn-fonipa">t͡ʂ, ʂ, ʐ</span>&#x27e9;</span>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEZygis2003_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEZygis2003-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Their actual phonetic realization is flat postalveolar <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[<a href="/wiki/Voiceless_retroflex_affricate" title="Voiceless retroflex affricate">t͡ʃ˖</a>,<span class="wrap"> </span><a href="/wiki/Voiceless_retroflex_fricative" title="Voiceless retroflex fricative">ʃ˖</a>,<span class="wrap"> </span><a href="/wiki/Voiced_retroflex_fricative" title="Voiced retroflex fricative">ʒ˖</a>]</span><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> in all of the Lower Sorbian-speaking area. This is unlike in standard Upper Sorbian, where these are <a href="/wiki/Palato-alveolar_consonant" title="Palato-alveolar consonant">palato-alveolar</a> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[<a href="/wiki/Voiceless_postalveolar_affricate" title="Voiceless postalveolar affricate">t͡ʃ</a>,<span class="wrap"> </span><a href="/wiki/Voiceless_postalveolar_fricative" title="Voiceless postalveolar fricative">ʃ</a>,<span class="wrap"> </span><a href="/wiki/Voiced_postalveolar_fricative" title="Voiced postalveolar fricative">ʒ</a>]</span>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEZygis2003180–181,_190–191_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEZygis2003180–181,_190–191-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEŠewc-Schuster198440–41_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEŠewc-Schuster198440–41-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Final_devoicing_and_assimilation">Final devoicing and assimilation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lower_Sorbian_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Final devoicing and assimilation"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Lower Sorbian has both <a href="/wiki/Final_devoicing" class="mw-redirect" title="Final devoicing">final devoicing</a> and regressive <a href="/wiki/Phonation" title="Phonation">voicing</a> <a href="/wiki/Assimilation_(linguistics)" class="mw-redirect" title="Assimilation (linguistics)">assimilation</a>:<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHannusch199812_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHannusch199812-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<ul><li><i>dub</i> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/dub/</span> "oak" is pronounced <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[dup]</span></li>
<li><i>susedka</i> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/ˈsusedka/</span> "(female) neighbor" is pronounced <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[ˈsusetka]</span></li>
<li><i>licba</i> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/ˈlit͡sba/</span> "number" is pronounced <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[ˈlʲid͡zba]</span></li></ul>
<p>The hard postalveolar fricative <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/ʃ/</span> is assimilated to <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[ɕ]</span> before <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/t͡ɕ/</span>:<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHannusch199813_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHannusch199813-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><i>šćit</i> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/ʃt͡ɕit/</span> "protection" is pronounced <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[ɕt͡ɕit]</span></li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vowels">Vowels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lower_Sorbian_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Vowels"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Stress">Stress</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lower_Sorbian_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Stress"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Stress_(linguistics)" title="Stress (linguistics)">Stress</a> in Lower Sorbian normally falls on the first <a href="/wiki/Syllable" title="Syllable">syllable</a> of the word:<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHannusch199814_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHannusch199814-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><span title="Lower Sorbian-language text"><i lang="dsb">Łužyca</i></span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[ˈwuʒɨt͡sa]</span> "<a href="/wiki/Lusatia" title="Lusatia">Lusatia</a>"</li>
<li><span title="Lower Sorbian-language text"><i lang="dsb">pśijaśel</i></span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[ˈpɕijaɕɛl]</span> "friend"</li>
<li><span title="Lower Sorbian-language text"><i lang="dsb">Chóśebuz</i></span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[ˈxɨɕɛbus]</span> "<a href="/wiki/Cottbus" title="Cottbus">Cottbus</a>"</li></ul>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Loanword" title="Loanword">loanwords</a>, stress may fall on any of the last three syllables:<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHannusch199814_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHannusch199814-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><span title="Lower Sorbian-language text"><i lang="dsb">internat</i></span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[intɛrˈnat]</span> "boarding school"</li>
<li><span title="Lower Sorbian-language text"><i lang="dsb">kontrola</i></span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[kɔnˈtrɔla]</span> "control"</li>
<li><span title="Lower Sorbian-language text"><i lang="dsb">september</i></span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[sɛpˈtɛmbɛr]</span> "September"</li>
<li><span title="Lower Sorbian-language text"><i lang="dsb">policija</i></span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[pɔˈlʲit͡sija]</span> "police"</li>
<li><span title="Lower Sorbian-language text"><i lang="dsb">organizacija</i></span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[ɔrɡanʲiˈzat͡sija]</span> "organization"</li></ul>
<p>Most one-syllable prepositions attract the stress to themselves when they precede a noun or pronoun of one or two syllables:<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHannusch199814_18-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHannusch199814-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><span title="Lower Sorbian-language text"><i lang="dsb">na dwórje</i></span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[ˈna<span class="wrap"> </span>dwɨrʲɛ]</span> "on the courtyard"</li>
<li><span title="Lower Sorbian-language text"><i lang="dsb">pśi mnjo</i></span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[ˈpɕi<span class="wrap"> </span>mnʲɔ]</span> "near me"</li>
<li><span title="Lower Sorbian-language text"><i lang="dsb">do města</i></span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[ˈdɔ<span class="wrap"> </span>mʲɛsta]</span> "into the city" (the <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[iɪ̯]</span> of <span title="Lower Sorbian-language text"><i lang="dsb">město</i></span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[ˈmʲiɪ̯stɔ]</span> becomes <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[ɛ]</span> when unstressed)</li></ul>
<p>However, nouns of three or more syllables retain their stress:
</p>
<ul><li><span title="Lower Sorbian-language text"><i lang="dsb">pśed wucabnikom</i></span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[pɕɛd<span class="wrap"> </span>ˈut͡sabnʲikɔm]</span> "in front of the teacher"</li>
<li><span title="Lower Sorbian-language text"><i lang="dsb">na drogowanju</i></span> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[na<span class="wrap"> </span>ˈdrɔɡowanʲu]</span> "on a journey"</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Orthography">Orthography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lower_Sorbian_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Orthography"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Sorbian_alphabet" title="Sorbian alphabet">Sorbian alphabet</a> is based on the <a href="/wiki/Latin_script" title="Latin script">Latin script</a> but uses <a href="/wiki/Diacritic" title="Diacritic">diacritics</a> such as the <a href="/wiki/Acute_accent" title="Acute accent">acute accent</a> and <a href="/wiki/Caron" title="Caron">caron</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sample">Sample</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lower_Sorbian_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Sample"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Article 1 of the <a href="/wiki/Universal_Declaration_of_Human_Rights" title="Universal Declaration of Human Rights">Universal Declaration of Human Rights</a> in Lower Sorbian:
</p>
<blockquote><p><span title="Lower Sorbian-language text"><i lang="dsb">Wšykne luźe su lichotne roźone a jadnake po dostojnosći a pšawach. Woni maju rozym a wědobnosć a maju ze sobu w duchu bratšojstwa wobchadaś.</i></span>
(All people are born free and equal in their dignity and rights. They are given reason and conscience and they shall create their relationships to one another according to the spirit of brotherhood.)<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
