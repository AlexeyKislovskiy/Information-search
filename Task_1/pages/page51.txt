<p><b>Tojol-ab'al</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Mayan_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Mayan language">Mayan language</a> spoken in <a href="/wiki/Chiapas" title="Chiapas">Chiapas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Tojolabal_people" title="Tojolabal people">Tojolabal people</a>. Tojol-ab'al is spoken, principally in the departments of the Chiapanecan Colonia of <a href="/wiki/Las_Margaritas,_Chiapas" title="Las Margaritas, Chiapas">Las Margaritas</a>, by about 70,000 people.<sup id="cite_ref-inegi2020_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inegi2020-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It is related to the <a href="/wiki/Chuj_language" title="Chuj language">Chuj language</a>.
</p><p>The name Tojolabal derives from the phrase <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[tohol<span class="wrap"> </span>aˈbal]</span>, meaning "right language".  Nineteenth-century documents sometimes refer to the language and its speakers as "Chaneabal" (meaning "four languages", possibly a reference to the four Mayan languages – <a href="/wiki/Tzotzil_language" title="Tzotzil language">Tzotzil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tzeltal_language" title="Tzeltal language">Tzeltal</a>, Tojolabal, and <a href="/wiki/Chuj_language" title="Chuj language">Chuj</a>—spoken in the Chiapas highlands and nearby lowlands along the Guatemala border).
</p><p>Anthropologist Carlos Lenkersdorf has claimed several linguistic and cultural features of the Tojolabal, primarily the language's  <a href="/wiki/Ergative-absolutive_languages" class="mw-redirect" title="Ergative-absolutive languages">ergativity</a>, show that they do not give cognitive weight to the distinctions subject/object, active/passive. This he interprets as being evidence in favor of the controversial <a href="/wiki/Linguistic_relativity" title="Linguistic relativity">Sapir-Whorf hypothesis</a>.
</p><p>The official <a href="/w/index.php?title=Writing_Standard_of_the_Tojol-ab%E2%80%99al_Language&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Writing Standard of the Tojol-ab’al Language (page does not exist)">Writing Standard of the Tojol-ab’al Language</a> (In Tojol-ab’al: <i><b>Skujlayub'il Sts'ijb'ajel K'umal Tojol-ab'al</b></i>, Spanish: <i>Norma de Escritura de la Lengua Tojol-ab’al</i>) was published in 2011 by the <a href="/wiki/Instituto_Nacional_de_Lenguas_Ind%C3%ADgenas" title="Instituto Nacional de Lenguas Indígenas">Instituto Nacional de Lenguas Indígenas</a>, used for indigenous education. It established an official alphabet, grammar rules and other linguistic aspects.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Tojol-abʼal-language programming is carried by the <a href="/wiki/National_Institute_of_Indigenous_Peoples" title="National Institute of Indigenous Peoples">National Institute of Indigenous Peoples</a> radio station <a href="/wiki/XEVFS" class="mw-redirect" title="XEVFS">XEVFS</a>, broadcasting from Las Margaritas.
</p>

<h2><span id="Sample_Text.5B4.5D.5B5.5D"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Sample_Text[4][5]">Sample Text<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tojolab%CA%BCal_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sample Text[4][5]"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="English">English</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tojolab%CA%BCal_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: English"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Article_1">Article 1</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tojolab%CA%BCal_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Article 1"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>All human beings are born free and equal in dignity and rights. They are endowed with reason and conscience and should act towards one another in a spirit of brotherhood.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Article_2">Article 2</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tojolab%CA%BCal_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Article 2"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Everyone is entitled to all the rights and freedoms set forth in this Declaration, without distinction of any kind, such as race, colour, sex, language, religion, political or other opinion, national or social origin, property, birth or other status. Furthermore, no distinction shall be made on the basis of the political, jurisdictional or international status of the country or territory to which a person belongs, whether it be independent, trust, non-self-governing or under any other limitation of sovereignty.
</p>
<h3><span id="Tojolab.27al"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Tojolab'al">Tojolab'al</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tojolab%CA%BCal_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Tojolab&#039;al"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Artikulo_1.">Artikulo 1.</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tojolab%CA%BCal_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Artikulo 1."><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Spetsanal ja swinkil ja lu’um k’inali junxta wax jul schonjel, sok ja sijpanub’ali, ja yuj ojni b’ob’ sk’u’luk ja jas sk’ana-i ja b’as lekilali, ja yuj ja ay sk’ujoli sok ay spensari t’ilan oj yilsb’aje lek sok ja smoj jumasa.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Artikulo_2.">Artikulo 2.</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tojolab%CA%BCal_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Artikulo 2."><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Spetsanal ja swinkil ja lu’um k’inali, ojni b’ob’ sk’uluk ja jas wax sk’ana-i sok sijpanub’alni ja jastal wax yala ja b’a ju’un iti, mini ma’ mas lek oj iljuk a’ma tuktukiluk ja yelawi, sok a’ma winikuk ma ixuk, cha a’nima tuktukiluk ja sk’umali, cha ja’chni ja jas wax sk’ani, sok ja spensari, ma jas tuk, ja b’a niwan chonab’ b’a’yi sok ja tsome b’a kulani, cha ja’chni a’mani me’y lek stak’in sok a’nima chikan jastal jul schonjel.
</p><p>Cha ja’chni spetsanal ja swinkil ja lu’um k’inali junxtani oj ilxuka a’ma tuktukiluk ja spensari sok ja sju’unil ja jas oj b’ob’ sk’uluki, ma ama tuktukiluk niwak chonab’ ja b’a’yi, cha a’ma jauk ja b’a slujmal b’a kulani, cha a’nima jun niwan chonab’ sijpanub’alxa ma jun slujmal b’a ay k’eluman ke titoni ay b’a sk’ab’ b’a jun niwan chonab’, ma oj kaltik jun niwan chonab’ b’a mito sijpanub’aluk.
</p>
