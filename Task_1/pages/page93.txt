</div></td></tr><tr><th colspan="2" class="infobox-header" style="color: black; background-color:  #c9ffd9;">Language codes</th></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label" style="white-space:nowrap;padding-right:0.65em;"><span class="nowrap"><a href="/wiki/ISO_639-3" title="ISO 639-3">ISO 639-3</a></span></th><td class="infobox-data" style="line-height:1.3em;"><code><a href="https://iso639-3.sil.org/code/trm" class="extiw" title="iso639-3:trm">trm</a></code></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label" style="white-space:nowrap;padding-right:0.65em;"><i><a href="/wiki/Glottolog" title="Glottolog">Glottolog</a></i></th><td class="infobox-data" style="line-height:1.3em;"><code><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://glottolog.org/resource/languoid/id/treg1243">treg1243</a></code></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label" style="white-space:nowrap;padding-right:0.65em;"><a href="/wiki/Endangered_Languages_Project" title="Endangered Languages Project">ELP</a></th><td class="infobox-data" style="line-height:1.3em;"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.endangeredlanguages.com/lang/3505">Tregami</a></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p><b>Tregami</b> (Trigami), or <i>Katar Gambiri</i>, is a language spoken in the villages of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gambir_(Afghanistan)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gambir (Afghanistan) (page does not exist)">Gambir</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Katar_(Afghanistan)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Katar (Afghanistan) (page does not exist)">Kaṭâr</a>, and Devoz in the Tregâm Valley off the lower <a href="/wiki/Pech_River" title="Pech River">Pech River</a><sup id="cite_ref-nuristani_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nuristani-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> in the <a href="/wiki/Watapur_District" title="Watapur District">Watapur District</a> of Kunar Province in Afghanistan. The area is in the <a href="/wiki/Hindu_Kush" title="Hindu Kush">Hindu Kush</a> along the border with Pakistan. Tregami belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Nuristani_languages" title="Nuristani languages">Nuristani</a> group of the <a href="/wiki/Indo-Iranian_languages" title="Indo-Iranian languages">Indo-Iranian</a> language family. It is spoken by approximately 3,500 people (2011).  Most individuals speak <a href="/wiki/Pashto" title="Pashto">Pashto</a> in addition to Tregami.<sup id="cite_ref-e18_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-e18-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Tregami is a close relative of <a href="/wiki/Waigali_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Waigali language">Waigali</a>, spoken in <a href="/wiki/Ghaziabad_District,_Kunar" title="Ghaziabad District, Kunar">Ghaziabad District</a> to the east, with which it has a lexical similarity of 75% to 80%.<sup id="cite_ref-e18_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-e18-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Although Tregami villages are close in proximity, there is a slight difference between the dialects of Katar and Gambir.<sup id="cite_ref-strand_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-strand-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The language has been influenced by the neighboring Indo-Aryan languages<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Avoid_weasel_words" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Avoid weasel words"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too vague attribution or weasel words. (May 2015)">which?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> and by the Nuristani <a href="/wiki/Kata-vari_dialect" title="Kata-vari dialect">Kata-vari dialect</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-iranic_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iranic-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tregami_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The native name is unknown. The <a href="/wiki/Exonym" class="mw-redirect" title="Exonym">exonym</a> <i>Tregâm</i>, from <a href="/wiki/Wotapuri-Katarqalai_language" title="Wotapuri-Katarqalai language">Wotapuri-Katarqalai</a>, literally means "three villages", referencing Gambir, Kaṭâr, and Devoz.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sociolinguistic_situation">Sociolinguistic situation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tregami_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sociolinguistic situation"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tregami is an unwritten<sup id="cite_ref-e18_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-e18-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Moribund_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Moribund language">moribund</a> language in the process of being <a href="/wiki/Language_shift" title="Language shift">replaced</a> by <a href="/wiki/Pashto" title="Pashto">Pashto</a>, the predominant language of the region. Most Tregami are bilingual in Pashto, and the Tregami people don't have the resources to revive their language.
</p>
