<p><b>Kawésqar</b> (Qawasqar), also known as <b>Alacaluf</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> is a critically endangered <a href="/wiki/Alacalufan_languages" title="Alacalufan languages">Alacalufan language</a> spoken in southern <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Alacalufe_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Alacalufe people">Kawésqar people</a>. Originally part of a <a href="/wiki/Alacalufan_languages" title="Alacalufan languages">small family</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> only the northern language remains. In 2009, only a handful of elderly people spoke the language, most of whom lived on <a href="/wiki/Wellington_Island" title="Wellington Island">Wellington Island</a> off the southwest coast of Chile.<sup id="cite_ref-EL_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EL-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Alphabet">Alphabet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kaw%C3%A9sqar_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Alphabet"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The alphabet in use has the following letters: a, æ, c, cꞌ, e, f, h, i, j, k, kꞌ, l, m, n, o, p, pꞌ, q, r, rr, s, t, tꞌ, u, w, x. However, differences are reported between dialects, and some sounds are not represented.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Morphology_and_syntax">Morphology and syntax</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kaw%C3%A9sqar_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Morphology and syntax"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kawésqar has a complex system of <a href="/wiki/Grammatical_tense" title="Grammatical tense">grammatical tense</a>, which includes a basic morphological contrast between future, present, immediate past, recent past, distant past, and mythological past events.
</p>
