<p><b>Puelche</b> was a language formerly spoken by the <a href="/wiki/Puelche_people" title="Puelche people">Puelche people</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Pampa" class="mw-redirect" title="Pampa">Pampas</a> region of <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>. The language is also known as <i>Gününa Küne</i>, Gennaken (Guenaken), Northern Tehuelche, <i>Gününa Yajich</i>, Ranquelche, and Pampa.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Classification">Classification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Puelche_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Classification"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Puelche has long been considered a <a href="/wiki/Language_isolate" title="Language isolate">language isolate</a>. Based on very limited evidence, Viegas Barros (1992) suggests that Puelche might be closely related to the language of the <a href="/wiki/Querand%C3%AD" title="Querandí">Querandí</a>, one of the <a href="/wiki/Het_peoples" title="Het peoples">Het peoples</a>, and Viegas Barros (2005) that it is related to the <a href="/wiki/Chonan_languages" title="Chonan languages">Chon languages</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Campbell-SAmerica_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Campbell-SAmerica-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Further afield, inclusion in a putative <a href="/wiki/Macro-Jibaro_languages" title="Macro-Jibaro languages">Macro-Jibaro</a> family has been posited.
</p>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:R%C3%ADo_Puelche,_afluente_Maule.JPG" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/R%C3%ADo_Puelche%2C_afluente_Maule.JPG/220px-R%C3%ADo_Puelche%2C_afluente_Maule.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/R%C3%ADo_Puelche%2C_afluente_Maule.JPG/330px-R%C3%ADo_Puelche%2C_afluente_Maule.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/R%C3%ADo_Puelche%2C_afluente_Maule.JPG/440px-R%C3%ADo_Puelche%2C_afluente_Maule.JPG 2x" data-file-width="4288" data-file-height="2848" /></a><figcaption>The <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Puelche" class="mw-redirect" title="Río Puelche">Puelche River</a>, named after the group, the river flows into the <a href="/wiki/Maule_River" title="Maule River">Maule River</a></figcaption></figure>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Documentation">Documentation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Puelche_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Documentation"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1829, <a href="/wiki/Alcide_d%27Orbigny" title="Alcide d&#39;Orbigny">D'Orbigny</a> toured the area of southern <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires_Province" title="Buenos Aires Province">Buenos Aires</a> and the mouth of the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Negro_(Argentina)" title="Río Negro (Argentina)">Río Negro</a>. There he collected words of the “puelches”, “<a href="/wiki/Mapuche_language" title="Mapuche language">aucas</a>” and “<a href="/wiki/Teushen_language" title="Teushen language">tehuelches</a>” —that is, in <b>günün a iajüch</b>, <b>mapuzungun</b> and <b>teushen</b>, respectively—in the vicinity of <a href="/wiki/Carmen_de_Patagones" title="Carmen de Patagones">Carmen de Patagones</a>, in a permanent settlement of linguistically heterogeneous groups.
</p><p>
The French traveler, intrigued by the indigenous languages of the area, arrived at the tolderías and contacted some interpreters: </p><blockquote><p>"<i>In a tent of friendly Patagonians I found a woman named Lunareja who spoke enough Spanish as if to serve as an interpreter; belonged to the Puelche nation and was married to a Patagonian, so that I knew both languages equally, which was of the greatest use to me. I also knew Araucanian, but the notions of this language could be better transmitted to me by the Indians.</i>"</p></blockquote><p>In <a href="/w/index.php?title=L%27Homme_am%C3%A9ricain&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="L&#39;Homme américain (page does not exist)">L'Homme américain</a> (de l'Amérique méridionale) he includes some comments on which highlights pronunciation features, the use of the morpheme ya- prefixed to the parts of the body, the numbering system or the absence of gender markings on adjectives, as well as a list of words that compares with those of
</p><p>other languages of South America. <sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
