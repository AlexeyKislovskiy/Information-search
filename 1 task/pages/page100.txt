<p><b>Karakalpak</b> (<span title="Karakalpak-language text"><i lang="kaa">Qaraqalpaq tili</i></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Turkic_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Turkic language">Turkic language</a> spoken by <a href="/wiki/Karakalpaks" title="Karakalpaks">Karakalpaks</a> in <a href="/wiki/Karakalpakstan" title="Karakalpakstan">Karakalpakstan</a>. It is divided into two dialects, Northeastern Karakalpak and Southwestern Karakalpak. It developed alongside <a href="/wiki/Nogai_language" title="Nogai language">Nogai</a> and neighbouring <a href="/wiki/Kazakh_language" title="Kazakh language">Kazakh languages</a>, being markedly influenced by both. Typologically, Karakalpak belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Kipchak_languages" title="Kipchak languages">Kipchak</a> branch of the Turkic languages, thus being closely related to and highly mutually intelligible with Kazakh and Nogai.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Classification">Classification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Karakalpak_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Classification"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Karakalpak is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Kipchak_languages" title="Kipchak languages">Kipchak</a> branch of <a href="/wiki/Turkic_languages" title="Turkic languages">Turkic languages</a>, which includes <a href="/wiki/Kazakh_language" title="Kazakh language">Kazakh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bashkir_language" title="Bashkir language">Bashkir</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tatar_language" title="Tatar language">Tatar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kumyk_language" title="Kumyk language">Kumyk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Karachay_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Karachay language">Karachay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nogai_language" title="Nogai language">Nogai</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kyrgyz_language" title="Kyrgyz language">Kyrgyz</a>. Due to its proximity to <a href="/wiki/Turkmen_language" title="Turkmen language">Turkmen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Uzbek_language" title="Uzbek language">Uzbek</a>, some of Karakalpak's vocabulary and grammar has been influenced by Uzbek and Turkmen. Like the vast majority of Turkic languages, Karakalpak has <a href="/wiki/Vowel_harmony" title="Vowel harmony">vowel harmony</a>, is <a href="/wiki/Agglutinative_language" title="Agglutinative language">agglutinative</a> and has no <a href="/wiki/Grammatical_gender" title="Grammatical gender">grammatical gender</a>. Word order is usually <a href="/wiki/Subject%E2%80%93object%E2%80%93verb" class="mw-redirect" title="Subject–object–verb">subject–object–verb</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geographic_distribution">Geographic distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Karakalpak_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geographic distribution"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Karakalpak is spoken mainly in the <a href="/wiki/Karakalpakstan" title="Karakalpakstan">Karakalpakstan</a> Autonomous Republic of <a href="/wiki/Uzbekistan" title="Uzbekistan">Uzbekistan</a>. Approximately 2,000 people in <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a> and smaller diaspora in parts of <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> and other parts of the world speak Karakalpak.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Official_status">Official status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Karakalpak_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Official status"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Karakalpak has official status in the Karakalpakstan Autonomous Republic.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dialects">Dialects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Karakalpak_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Dialects"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Ethnologue" title="Ethnologue">Ethnologue</a></i> identifies two dialects of Karakalpak: Northeastern and Southwestern. <a href="/wiki/Karl_Heinrich_Menges" title="Karl Heinrich Menges">Menges</a> mentions a third possible dialect spoken in the <a href="/wiki/Fergana_Valley" title="Fergana Valley">Fergana Valley</a>.  The Southwestern dialect has /tʃ/ for the Northeastern /ʃ/.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Phonology">Phonology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Karakalpak_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Phonology"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Writing_system">Writing system</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Karakalpak_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Writing system"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<figure typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Suwretxana.JPG" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Suwretxana.JPG/300px-Suwretxana.JPG" decoding="async" width="300" height="225" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Suwretxana.JPG/450px-Suwretxana.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Suwretxana.JPG/600px-Suwretxana.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2048" data-file-height="1536" /></a><figcaption>March 2006. A photo laboratory in <a href="/wiki/Nukus" title="Nukus">Nukus</a> – with the signboard written in Karakalpak language using the Latin alphabet.</figcaption></figure>
<p>Karakalpak was written in the <a href="/wiki/Arabic_script" title="Arabic script">Arabic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Persian_alphabet" title="Persian alphabet">Persian script</a> until 1932, in the <a href="/wiki/Latin_script" title="Latin script">Latin script</a> from 1928 to 1940, after which <a href="/wiki/Cyrillic_script" title="Cyrillic script">Cyrillic</a> was introduced. Following Uzbekistan's independence in 1991, the decision was made to drop Cyrillic and revert to the Latin alphabet. Whilst the use of Latin script is now widespread in <a href="/wiki/Tashkent" title="Tashkent">Tashkent</a>, its introduction into Karakalpakstan remains gradual.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Cyrillic, Latin, and Arabic alphabets are shown below with their equivalent representations in the IPA. Cyrillic letters with no representation in the Latin alphabet are marked with asterisks. The last changes to the new Karakalpak alphabet were made in 2016: instead of letters with apostrophes, letters with acutes were introduced.<sup id="cite_ref-лотин_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-лотин-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
