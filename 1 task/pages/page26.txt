<p><b>Enets</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Samoyedic_languages" title="Samoyedic languages">Samoyedic language</a> of Northern Siberia spoken on the Lower <a href="/wiki/Yenisei" class="mw-redirect" title="Yenisei">Yenisei</a> within the boundaries of the <a href="/wiki/Taymyrsky_Dolgano-Nenetsky_District" title="Taymyrsky Dolgano-Nenetsky District">Taimyr</a> Municipality District, a subdivision of <a href="/wiki/Krasnoyarsk_Krai" title="Krasnoyarsk Krai">Krasnoyarsk Krai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia Federation</a>.  Enets belongs to the Northern branch of the <a href="/wiki/Samoyedic_languages" title="Samoyedic languages">Samoyedic languages</a>, in turn a branch of the <a href="/wiki/Uralic_languages" title="Uralic languages">Uralic</a> language family.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In 2010 about 40 people claimed to be native Enets speakers, while In 2020, 69 people claimed to speak Enets natively, while 97 people answered to know Enets in total.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Dialects">Dialects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enets_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Dialects"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are two distinct dialects, Forest Enets (Bai) and Tundra Enets (Madu or Somatu), which may be considered separate languages.
</p><p>Forest Enets is the smaller of the two Enets dialects. In the winter of 2006/2007, approximately 35 people spoke it (6 in <a href="/wiki/Dudinka" title="Dudinka">Dudinka</a>, 20 in <a href="/w/index.php?title=Potapova,_Russia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Potapova, Russia (page does not exist)">Potapova</a> and 10 in <a href="/wiki/Tukhard" title="Tukhard">Tukhard</a>, the youngest of whom was born in 1962 and the oldest in 1945). Many of these speakers are <a href="/wiki/Multilingualism" title="Multilingualism">trilingual</a>, with competence in Forest Enets, <a href="/wiki/Tundra_Nenets_language" title="Tundra Nenets language">Tundra Nenets</a> and <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>, preferring to speak Tundra Nenets.
</p><p>The two dialects differ both in phonology and in lexicon. Additional variation was found in early Enets records from the 17th to 19th centuries, though all these varieties can be assigned as either Tundra Enets or Forest Enets.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Phonological differences:
</p>
<ul><li>In some words, Forest Enets <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/s/</span> corresponds to Tundra Enets <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/ɟ/</span> (from Proto-Samoyedic *ms, *ns, *rs and *rkʲ).
<ul><li>Forest <span title="Forest Enets-language text"><i lang="enf-Latn">mese</i></span> — Tundra <span title="Tundra Enets-language text"><i lang="enh-Latn">meɟe</i></span> 'wind' (from *merse &lt; *märkʲä);</li>
<li>Forest <span title="Forest Enets-language text"><i lang="enf-Latn">osa</i></span> — Tundra <span title="Tundra Enets-language text"><i lang="enh-Latn">uɟa</i></span> 'meat' (from *ʊnsa &lt; *əmså);</li>
<li>Forest <span title="Forest Enets-language text"><i lang="enf-Latn">sira</i></span> — Tundra <span title="Tundra Enets-language text"><i lang="enh-Latn">silra</i></span> 'snow';</li>
<li>Forest <span title="Forest Enets-language text"><i lang="enf-Latn">judado</i></span> — Tundra <span title="Tundra Enets-language text"><i lang="enh-Latn">judaro</i></span> 'pike';</li>
<li>Forest <span title="Forest Enets-language text"><i lang="enf-Latn">kadaʔa</i></span> — Tundra <span title="Tundra Enets-language text"><i lang="enh-Latn">karaʔa</i></span> 'grandmother';<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></li></ul></li>
<li>In some words, Forest Enets word-initial <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/na/</span> corresponds to Tundra Enets <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/e/</span> (from Proto-Samoyedic *a- &gt; *ä-).</li>
<li>Certain vowel + glide sequences of Proto-Samoyedic have different reflexes in Forest Enets and Tundra Enets.</li>
<li>Forest Enets word-initial <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/ɟi/</span> corresponds to Tundra Enets <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/i/</span>.</li></ul>
<p>Lexical differences:
</p>
<ul><li>Forest <span title="Forest Enets-language text"><i lang="enf-Latn">eba</i></span> — Tundra <span title="Tundra Enets-language text"><i lang="enh-Latn">aburi</i></span> 'head'</li>
<li>Forest <span title="Forest Enets-language text"><i lang="enf-Latn">baða</i></span> — Tundra <span title="Tundra Enets-language text"><i lang="enh-Latn">nau</i></span> 'word'</li>
<li>Forest <span title="Forest Enets-language text"><i lang="enf-Latn">ʃaru</i></span> — Tundra <span title="Tundra Enets-language text"><i lang="enh-Latn">oma</i></span> 'tobacco'</li>
<li>Forest <span title="Forest Enets-language text"><i lang="enf-Latn">abbua</i></span> — Tundra <span title="Tundra Enets-language text"><i lang="enh-Latn">miʔ</i></span> 'what'<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>

<p>The written form of the Enets language was created during the 1980s and has been used to produce a number of books. During the 1990s there was a local newspaper with insert in local languages (including Enets language), Советский Таймыр (Soviet Taimyr, modern simple <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taymyr_(newspaper)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Taymyr (newspaper) (page does not exist)">Taymyr</a></i>) published and brief Enets broadcasts on local radio, which shut down in 2003,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> served as supplements for speakers.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Stress">Stress</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enets_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Stress"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The type of stress in Enets is quantitative. Stressed vowels are pronounced relatively longer than unstressed vowels. Based on the available data, the stress is not (as a rule) used as a feature for distinguishing the meaning. The stress in a word usually falls on the first vowel. The primary stress usually falls on the first syllable and is accompanied by a secondary stress, which falls on the third and the fifth syllable. Sometimes the stress distinguishes the meaning, e.g. in mo·di ('I') vs. modi· ('shoulder'). (The primary stress is marked by ·).<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Morphology">Morphology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enets_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Morphology"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The parts of speech in Enets are: nouns, adjectives, numerals, pronouns, verbs, adverbs, postpositions, conjunctions, interjections and connective particles.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The grammatical number is expressed by means of the opposition of the singular, dual and plural forms. There are three declensions, the main (non-possessive), possessive and desiderative declensions, and seven cases in Enets: the nominative, genitive, accusative, lative, locative, ablative and prolative case. The meaning of those cases is expressed by means of suffixes added to nouns, adjectives, pronouns and substantivized verbs. In their fixed forms they also belong to adverbs and postpositions. The possession is expressed by means of the genitive case or possessive suffixes.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Local orientation is based on the three-member distribution: the suffixes of local cases of nouns, adverbs and postpositions are divided among the <a href="/wiki/Lative_case" title="Lative case">lative</a> (<i>to where?</i>), <a href="/wiki/Locative_case" title="Locative case">locative</a> (<i>where?</i>) and <a href="/wiki/Ablative_case" title="Ablative case">ablative</a> (<i>from where?</i>). The <a href="/wiki/Prolative_case" title="Prolative case">prolative</a> case (<i>along what?</i> or <i>through what?</i>) expresses an additional fourth local characteristic.
</p><p>The verbal negation is expressed by the combination of the main verb with a preceding auxiliary negative verb. The auxiliary verb is conjugated according to general rules, but the main verb is in a special inconjugated negative form. There are also some verbs of absence - non-possessiveness. Six moods are contrasted in the Enets language: indicative, conjunctive, imperative, optative, quotative and interrogative.  There are three tenses: <a href="/wiki/Aorist" title="Aorist">aorist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Preterite" title="Preterite">preterite</a> and <a href="/wiki/Future_tense" title="Future tense">future</a>. <sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The category of person with nouns is expressed by means of possessive suffixes, differing in all three numbers of all three persons and used in nouns, pronouns, substantivized verbs, adverbs and postpositions. The category of person with verbs is expressed by means of particular personal suffixes of the verb, differing in all three numbers of all three persons.
</p><p>There are three conjugations in Enets: subjective, objective and reflexive. These conjugations differ from each other by personal suffixes. Additionally, the objective conjugation uses numerical suffixes, referring to all three numbers of the object. In the case of the reflexive conjugation, the person of the subject and object is the same and a separate suffix indicates reflexivity.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nouns">Nouns</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enets_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Nouns"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Depending on the final sounds of the word stem, nouns can be divided into two groups: 
</p>
<ol><li>nouns with a final sound other than a laryngal plosive stop, e.g. <i>d'uda</i> 'horse'</li>
<li>nouns with a final laryngal plosive stop, e.g. <i>tauʔ</i> 'Nganasan'</li></ol>
<p>Either group uses variants of suffixes with a different initial sound (e.g. Loc <i>d'uda-han</i>, <i>tau-kon</i>).
</p><p>There are seven cases in Enets: the nominative, genitive, accusative, lative, locative, ablative and prolative case. The case suffixes are combined with numeral markers, often in a fairly complex manner.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Adjectives">Adjectives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enets_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Adjectives"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There are a number of adjectives that have no specific suffixes, e.g. <i>utik</i> 'bad', <i>sojδa</i> 'good', <i>lodo</i> 'low' and <i>piδe</i> 'high'.
</p><p>Alongside of these there are various suffixal adjectives, e.g. <i>buse̮-saj ne̮</i> 'a married woman', <i>bite-δa</i> 'waterless', <i>uδa-šiδa</i> 'handless', <i>mȯga-he</i> 'belonging to the forest', <i>same-raha</i> 'wolf-like', <i>narδe-de̮</i> 'red', <i>polδe-de̮</i> 'black'.
</p><p>An adjective does not agree with the following main word either in number or case, e.g. <i>agga koja</i> 'big sterlet', <i>agga koja-hone</i> (locative), <i>agga koja-hi̮t</i> (plural ablative). As an exception , we can refer to the use of the adjective instead of an elliptical noun and as a predicate in the nominal conjugation.
</p><p>With the aim of strengthening a possessive connection, sometimes a respective possessive suffix may be added to the main word of an attribute, e.g. <i><u>keδerʔ</u> <b>koba-δa</b> ŋul'ʔ mujuʔ</i> 'the wild reindeer skin is very strong' ("<b>its-skin</b> <u>of-the-wild-reindeer</u>...").
</p><p>The comparative degree is formed by means of an adjective in the positive degree (in the nominative form) with the word to be compared in the ablative form.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_numerals">Other numerals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enets_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Other numerals"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Collective numerals are formed combining a separate word namely a form e̮š of the auxiliary verb 'to be' with cardinal numerals, e.g. siδe e̮š 'we two, the two of us'.
</p><p>Distributive numerals are postpositional constructions of cardinals, combined with the postposition loδ, e.g. siδeʔ loδ 'by (in) twos'.
</p><p>Iteratives are the plural forms of cardinals, e.g. ŋobuʔ 'one time, once'.
</p><p>Fractional numerals are cardinals that are combined with the word boʔ 'a half', e.g. nehuʔ boʔ 'one-third'.
</p><p>Temporal numerals are formed from cardinals by means of the suffix -ʔ, e.g. orδede̮ʔ 'the first time'.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_pronouns">Other pronouns</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enets_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Other pronouns"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Reflexive pronouns are pairs of words whose first component consists of personal pronouns, the second is a separate word stem <i>ker-</i>, combined with their respective possessive suffixes, e.g. <i>mod' keriń</i> 'I myself', <i>ū kerit</i> 'you yourself', <i>bu kerta</i> 'she herself/he himself' or <i>modiń keriń</i> 'we two ourselves'.
</p><p>Interrogative pronouns are <i>kurse̮</i> 'which?', <i>sēa</i> 'who?' (used only for humans) and obu 'what?' (used for animals and lifeless objects).
</p><p>Negative pronouns are formed from interrogative pronouns by adding the suffix -hȯru, e.g. obuhȯru.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Verbs">Verbs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enets_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Verbs"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The verbs in Enets can be distributed into two groups in principally the same manner as the noun depending on the final sounds of the word stem. Either group uses the variants of suffixes with different initial sounds.
</p><p>Seven moods are contrasted: indicative, conjunctive, imperative, optative, quotative and interrogative. There are three tenses: aorist, preterite and future. (These tenses exist practically only in the indicative mood.) The verb has three conjugations: subjective, objective and reflexive. These conjugations differ from each other by personal suffixes. In addition to this the objective conjugation uses numerical suffixes, referring to all three numbers of the object. In the case of reflexive conjugation a separate suffix indicates reflexivity.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Finite_forms">Finite forms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enets_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Finite forms"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The aorist is either unmarked or with the marker <i>-ŋV-/-V-</i>. The temporal meaning of the aorist depends on the aspect of the verb. A prolonged or recurrent action should be understood as taking place in the present, a short-time or single action as having taken place in the past, whereas the influence of the latter is still felt in the present. A distinctly past action is expressed by the preterite with the marker <i>-ś/-š/-d'/-t'/-č</i>, whereas the marker is placed after personal suffixes. The future action is expressed by the future marker <i>-d-/-dV-/-t-/-tV-</i> before personal suffixes.
</p><p>The objective conjugation uses one type of personal suffixes when the object is in the singular and another type of them with the object in the dual or the plural. In the case of the dual object the dual marker <i>-hu-/-gu-/-ku-</i> precedes the dual personal suffixes of the second type, whereas in the case of the plural object, the rise of the stem vowel can be observed. The marker of the reflexive mood is <i>-i-</i>, which is standing before personal suffixes.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Syntax">Syntax</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enets_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Syntax"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Syntax" title="Syntax">syntax</a> of Enets is typical for the family and the area. The Enets language follows <a href="/wiki/Subject-object-verb" class="mw-redirect" title="Subject-object-verb">Subject-object-verb</a>, <a href="/wiki/Head_marking" class="mw-redirect" title="Head marking">head marking</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Noun_phrase" title="Noun phrase">noun phrase</a>, both head and <a href="/wiki/Dependent_marking" class="mw-redirect" title="Dependent marking">dependent marking</a> within the clause, non-finite verbal forms used for clause combining. Consequently, the finite verb form (the <a href="/wiki/Predicate_(grammar)" title="Predicate (grammar)">predicate</a>) is always at the end of a sentence. The negative auxiliary verb immediately precedes the main verb. The object of a sentence always keeps to the word it belongs to.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Grammar">Grammar</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enets_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Grammar"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Enets nouns vary for number, case, and person-number of the possessor. There is also an intriguing nominal case in which ‘destinativity’ determines the entity is destined for someone. Possessor markers are also used for discourse related purposes, where they are completely devoid of the literal possessive meaning. Enets postpositions are marked for person-number; many postpositions are formed from a small set of relational nouns and case morphology.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
