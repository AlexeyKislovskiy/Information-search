<p><b>Kui</b> (କୁଇ)(also Kandh, Khondi, Khond, Khondo, Kanda, Kodu (Kōdu), Kodulu, Kuinga (Kūinga), Kuy) is a South-Central <a href="/wiki/Dravidian_languages" title="Dravidian languages">Dravidian</a> language spoken by the <a href="/wiki/Khonds" title="Khonds">Kandhas</a>, eastern Indian state of Odisha. It is mostly spoken in <a href="/wiki/Odisha" title="Odisha">Odisha</a>, and written in the <a href="/wiki/Odia_script" title="Odia script">Odia script</a>. With 941,988 registered native speakers, it figures at <a href="/wiki/List_of_Indian_languages_by_number_of_native_speakers" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Indian languages by number of native speakers">rank 29</a> in the 1991 <a href="/wiki/Indian_census" class="mw-redirect" title="Indian census">Indian census</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The Kui language was also referred to as the Kuinga language during the historical period. It is closely related to the <a href="/wiki/Gondi_language" title="Gondi language">Gondi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kuvi_language" title="Kuvi language">Kuvi</a> languages.
</p>
