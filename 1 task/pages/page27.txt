<p><b>Megleno-Romanian</b> (known as <span title="Megleno Romanian-language text"><i lang="ruq">vlăhește</i></span> by its speakers, and Megleno-Romanian or <b>Meglenitic</b> and sometimes <b>Moglenitic</b> or <b>Meglinitic</b> by linguists) is an <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Romance_languages" title="Eastern Romance languages">Eastern Romance language</a>, similar to <a href="/wiki/Aromanian_language" title="Aromanian language">Aromanian</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> It is spoken by the <a href="/wiki/Megleno-Romanians" title="Megleno-Romanians">Megleno-Romanians</a> in a few villages in the <a href="/wiki/Moglena" class="mw-redirect" title="Moglena">Moglena</a> region that spans the border between the <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greek</a> region of <a href="/wiki/Macedonia_(Greece)" title="Macedonia (Greece)">Macedonia</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_Macedonia" title="North Macedonia">North Macedonia</a>. It is also spoken by emigrants from these villages and their descendants in <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> by a small <a href="/wiki/Muslim" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> group, and in <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a>. It is considered an <a href="/wiki/Endangered_language" title="Endangered language">endangered language</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Classification">Classification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Megleno-Romanian_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Classification"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Megleno-Romanian is a member of the family of Romance languages. More specifically, it is an <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Romance_languages" title="Eastern Romance languages">Eastern Romance language</a>, a language formed after the retreat of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a> from the Balkans. Due to the fact that it is spoken by very few people and because of its similarities with the <a href="/wiki/Aromanian_language" title="Aromanian language">Aromanian</a>, modern <a href="/wiki/Romanian_language" title="Romanian language">Romanian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Istro-Romanian_language" title="Istro-Romanian language">Istro-Romanian</a> languages, some linguists consider it to be an intermediary between <a href="/wiki/Romanian_language" title="Romanian language">Romanian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aromanian_language" title="Aromanian language">Aromanian</a>, often being considered either a dialect of Romanian, a dialect of Aromanian, or an independent language. It is closer to standard Romanian than the Aromanian language, suggesting that it split from <a href="/wiki/Common_Romanian" title="Common Romanian">Common Romanian</a> later than Aromanian. Megleno-Romanian has been strongly influenced by the neighbouring <a href="/wiki/South_Slavic_languages" title="South Slavic languages">South Slavic</a> varieties.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Megleno-Romanian_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Name"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The term <i>Megleno-Romanian</i> has been used by linguists (mainly Romanians), who noticed the similarity to the <a href="/wiki/Romanian_language" title="Romanian language">Romanian language</a>. The Megleno-Romanians identify themselves as <span title="Megleno Romanian-language text"><i lang="ruq">vlaș</i></span> ("<a href="/wiki/Vlach" class="mw-redirect" title="Vlach">Vlach</a>") or by local endonyms such as <span title="Megleno Romanian-language text"><i lang="ruq">liumnicean</i></span> ("from <a href="/wiki/Skra,_Kilkis" title="Skra, Kilkis">Liumnița</a>") or <span title="Megleno Romanian-language text"><i lang="ruq">umineț</i></span> ("from <a href="/wiki/Huma,_North_Macedonia" title="Huma, North Macedonia">Uma</a>").<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geographical_distribution">Geographical distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Megleno-Romanian_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geographical distribution"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Megleno-Romanian is spoken in several villages in the <a href="/wiki/Pella_(regional_unit)" title="Pella (regional unit)">Pella</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kilkis_(regional_unit)" title="Kilkis (regional unit)">Kilkis</a> regional units of <a href="/wiki/Macedonia_(Greece)" title="Macedonia (Greece)">Macedonia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>, as well as in a handful of villages across the border in <a href="/wiki/North_Macedonia" title="North Macedonia">North Macedonia</a>. In the village of <a href="/wiki/Huma,_North_Macedonia" title="Huma, North Macedonia">Huma</a>, the language was spoken by most inhabitants before they and other Megleno-Romanians from the region moved in the cities of <a href="/wiki/Gevgelija" title="Gevgelija">Gevgelija</a> and <a href="/wiki/Skopje" title="Skopje">Skopje</a> where some have preserved their native language.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> After <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>, some Megleno-Romanians moved to <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Southern_Dobruja" title="Southern Dobruja">Southern Dobruja</a>, but were moved to the village of <a href="/wiki/Cerna,_Tulcea" title="Cerna, Tulcea">Cerna</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tulcea_County" title="Tulcea County">Tulcea County</a> (<a href="/wiki/Northern_Dobruja" title="Northern Dobruja">Northern Dobruja</a>) after the <a href="/wiki/Population_exchange_between_Bulgaria_and_Romania" title="Population exchange between Bulgaria and Romania">population exchange between Bulgaria and Romania</a>. In Cerna, about 1,200 people continue to speak Megleno-Romanian. In 1940, about 30 families moved from Cerna to the <a href="/wiki/Banat" title="Banat">Banat</a> region of <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a> in the villages of Variaș, Biled and Jimbolia. Some speakers who identified as <a href="/wiki/Muslim" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim">Muslim</a>, from the village of <a href="/wiki/N%C3%A2nti" class="mw-redirect" title="Nânti">Nânti</a> (Nótia), were moved to Turkey from Greece as part of the <a href="/wiki/Population_exchange_between_Greece_and_Turkey" title="Population exchange between Greece and Turkey">population exchange</a> between them of the 1920s.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Some also live in <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a>, specially in the village of <a href="/wiki/Gudurica" title="Gudurica">Gudurica</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Phonology">Phonology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Megleno-Romanian_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Phonology"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Megleno-Romanian is not a standardised language and there are phonological differences across idioms.<sup id="cite_ref-synthese_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-synthese-11">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Some particular phonetic characteristics of the Megleno-Romanian vowel system compared to other <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Romance_languages" title="Eastern Romance languages">Eastern Romance languages</a> are:<sup id="cite_ref-synthese_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-synthese-11">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ol><li>long vowels: ā, ē, ī, ǭ, ō, ū</li>
<li>use of the <a href="/wiki/Open-mid_back_rounded_vowel" title="Open-mid back rounded vowel">open-mid back rounded vowel</a> [ɔ] in some words which in Romanian would use [ə] and [ɨ], for example: <span title="Megleno Romanian-language text"><span lang="ruq" style="font-style: normal;">pǫ́nză</span></span></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Apheresis_(linguistics)" title="Apheresis (linguistics)">apheresis</a> of [a] in initial position: <span title="Romanian-language text"><span lang="ro" style="font-style: normal;">aveam</span></span> → <span title="Megleno Romanian-language text"><span lang="ruq" style="font-style: normal;">veam</span></span> ("we had"), <span title="Romanian-language text"><span lang="ro" style="font-style: normal;">aduc</span></span> → <span title="Megleno Romanian-language text"><span lang="ruq" style="font-style: normal;">duc</span></span> ("I bring")</li></ol>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Vocabulary">Vocabulary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Megleno-Romanian_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Vocabulary"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<figure typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:%D0%92%D0%B8%D0%BA%D0%B8%D0%B5%D0%BA%D1%81%D0%BF%D0%B5%D0%B4%D0%B8%D1%86%D0%B8%D1%98%D0%B0_%D0%91%D0%BE%D1%98%D0%BC%D0%B8%D1%98%D0%B0_023.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/%D0%92%D0%B8%D0%BA%D0%B8%D0%B5%D0%BA%D1%81%D0%BF%D0%B5%D0%B4%D0%B8%D1%86%D0%B8%D1%98%D0%B0_%D0%91%D0%BE%D1%98%D0%BC%D0%B8%D1%98%D0%B0_023.jpg/250px-%D0%92%D0%B8%D0%BA%D0%B8%D0%B5%D0%BA%D1%81%D0%BF%D0%B5%D0%B4%D0%B8%D1%86%D0%B8%D1%98%D0%B0_%D0%91%D0%BE%D1%98%D0%BC%D0%B8%D1%98%D0%B0_023.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="166" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/%D0%92%D0%B8%D0%BA%D0%B8%D0%B5%D0%BA%D1%81%D0%BF%D0%B5%D0%B4%D0%B8%D1%86%D0%B8%D1%98%D0%B0_%D0%91%D0%BE%D1%98%D0%BC%D0%B8%D1%98%D0%B0_023.jpg/375px-%D0%92%D0%B8%D0%BA%D0%B8%D0%B5%D0%BA%D1%81%D0%BF%D0%B5%D0%B4%D0%B8%D1%86%D0%B8%D1%98%D0%B0_%D0%91%D0%BE%D1%98%D0%BC%D0%B8%D1%98%D0%B0_023.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/%D0%92%D0%B8%D0%BA%D0%B8%D0%B5%D0%BA%D1%81%D0%BF%D0%B5%D0%B4%D0%B8%D1%86%D0%B8%D1%98%D0%B0_%D0%91%D0%BE%D1%98%D0%BC%D0%B8%D1%98%D0%B0_023.jpg/500px-%D0%92%D0%B8%D0%BA%D0%B8%D0%B5%D0%BA%D1%81%D0%BF%D0%B5%D0%B4%D0%B8%D1%86%D0%B8%D1%98%D0%B0_%D0%91%D0%BE%D1%98%D0%BC%D0%B8%D1%98%D0%B0_023.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4928" data-file-height="3264" /></a><figcaption>Megleno-Romanian inscription (<span title="Megleno Romanian-language text"><i lang="ruq">Ceshma ămpiratului</i></span>, "the Emperor's Fountain") on a water fountain along the way to <a href="/wiki/Huma,_North_Macedonia" title="Huma, North Macedonia">Huma</a>, a village in North Macedonia</figcaption></figure>
<p>Much of the vocabulary is of <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> origin and much of its phonetics and semantics is shared with Aromanian and Romanian: (n.b.: MR=Megleno-Romanian, DR=Daco-Romanian, i.e. Romanian)
</p>
<ul><li><span title="Latin-language text"><i lang="la">basilica</i></span> &gt; MR <b><span title="Megleno Romanian-language text"><i lang="ruq">bisearică</i></span></b>, DR <span title="Romanian-language text"><i lang="ro">biserică</i></span> (church, originally "<a href="/wiki/Basilica" title="Basilica">basilica</a>")</li>
<li><span title="Latin-language text"><i lang="la">lumen</i></span> &gt; MR <b><span title="Megleno Romanian-language text"><i lang="ruq">lumi</i></span></b>, DR <span title="Romanian-language text"><i lang="ro">lume</i></span> (world, originally "<a href="/wiki/Light" title="Light">light</a>")</li>
<li><span title="Latin-language text"><i lang="la">monumentum</i></span> &gt; MR <b><span title="Megleno Romanian-language text"><i lang="ruq">murmint</i></span></b>, DR <span title="Romanian-language text"><i lang="ro">mormânt</i></span> (grave, originally "<a href="/wiki/Monument" title="Monument">monument</a>")</li>
<li><span title="Latin-language text"><i lang="la">strigis</i></span> &gt; MR <b><span title="Megleno Romanian-language text"><i lang="ruq">strig</i></span></b>, DR <span title="Romanian-language text"><i lang="ro">strig</i></span> (I yell, originally "<a href="/wiki/Owl" title="Owl">owl</a>")</li>
<li><span title="Latin-language text"><i lang="la">draco</i></span> &gt; MR <b><span title="Megleno Romanian-language text"><i lang="ruq">drac</i></span></b>, DR <span title="Romanian-language text"><i lang="ro">drac</i></span> (devil, originally "<a href="/wiki/Dragon" title="Dragon">dragon</a>")</li></ul>
<p>Megleno-Romanian also contains some words that have cognates with <a href="/wiki/Albanian_language" title="Albanian language">Albanian</a>. These words are present in <a href="/wiki/Daco-Romanian" class="mw-redirect" title="Daco-Romanian">Daco-Romanian</a> too:
</p>
<ul><li>MR <b><span title="Megleno Romanian-language text"><i lang="ruq">brad</i></span></b>; DR <span title="Romanian-language text"><i lang="ro">brad</i></span>; cf. Alb. <span title="Albanian-language text"><i lang="sq">bredh</i></span> (fir tree)</li>
<li>MR <b><span title="Megleno Romanian-language text"><i lang="ruq">monz</i></span></b>; DR <span title="Romanian-language text"><i lang="ro">mânz</i></span>; cf. Alb. <span title="Albanian-language text"><i lang="sq">mës</i></span> (colt)</li>
<li>MR <b><span title="Megleno Romanian-language text"><i lang="ruq">bucuros</i></span></b>; DR <span title="Romanian-language text"><i lang="ro">bucuros</i></span>; (happy) cf. Alb <span title="Albanian-language text"><i lang="sq">bukur</i></span> (beautiful)</li></ul>
<p>There are also some words which are of Slavic origin and which can be found in all the Eastern Romance languages:
</p>
<ul><li>MR <b><span title="Megleno Romanian-language text"><i lang="ruq">stăpân</i></span></b>; DR <span title="Romanian-language text"><i lang="ro">trup</i></span> (body); cf. Sl. <i>trupŭ</i></li>
<li>MR <b><span title="Megleno Romanian-language text"><i lang="ruq">stăpon</i></span></b>; DR <span title="Romanian-language text"><i lang="ro">stăpân</i></span> (master); cf. Old Slavic. <i>stopanŭ</i>, today's Bulgarian <span title="Bulgarian-language text"><i lang="bg-Latn">stopanin</i></span> and Macedonian <span title="Macedonian-language text"><i lang="mk-Latn">stopan</i></span></li></ul>
<p>There are a number of <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Greek" class="mw-redirect" title="Byzantine Greek">Byzantine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Modern_Greek" title="Modern Greek">Modern Greek</a> words, several dozens of which are also found in <a href="/wiki/Daco-Romanian" class="mw-redirect" title="Daco-Romanian">Daco-Romanian</a> (<a href="/wiki/Romanian_language" title="Romanian language">Romanian language</a>) and Aromanian and about 80 words that were borrowed via <a href="/wiki/Macedonian_language" title="Macedonian language">Macedonian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_language" title="Bulgarian language">Bulgarian</a> languages and other languages of the <a href="/wiki/Balkans" title="Balkans">Balkans</a>. Prior to the creation of the modern state of Greece, Megleno-Romanian borrowed very few words directly from Greek.
</p>
<ul><li>Gr. <span title="Greek-language text"><i lang="el-Latn">prósfatos</i></span> &gt; MR <b><span title="Megleno Romanian-language text"><i lang="ruq">proaspit</i></span></b>; DR <span title="Romanian-language text"><i lang="ro">proaspăt</i></span> (fresh)</li>
<li>Gr. <span title="Greek-language text"><i lang="el-Latn">keramídi</i></span> &gt; MR <b><span title="Megleno Romanian-language text"><i lang="ruq">chirămidă</i></span></b>; DR <span title="Romanian-language text"><i lang="ro">cărămidă</i></span> (brick)</li>
<li>Gr. <span title="Greek-language text"><i lang="el-Latn">lemoni</i></span> &gt; MR <b><span title="Megleno Romanian-language text"><i lang="ruq">limonă</i></span></b>, via Bulg. <span title="Bulgarian-language text"><i lang="bg-Latn">limon</i></span> (lemon); cf. DR <span title="Romanian-language text"><i lang="ro">lămâie</i></span></li></ul>
<p>The most important influence on Megleno-Romanian was the <a href="/wiki/East_South_Slavic_languages" class="mw-redirect" title="East South Slavic languages">East South Slavic languages</a>, this influence being more profound than that exerted by Greek on Aromanian. Most Slavic terms are of <a href="/wiki/Macedonian_language" title="Macedonian language">Macedonian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_language" title="Bulgarian language">Bulgarian</a> origins. The linguist <a href="/wiki/Theodor_Capidan" title="Theodor Capidan">Theodor Capidan</a> argued that the words borrowed show some phonetic features of the Bulgarian language dialect spoken in the <a href="/wiki/Rhodope_Mountains" title="Rhodope Mountains">Rhodope Mountains</a>. There are many instances where basic words of Latin origin that can still be found in Daco-Romanian and Aromanian were replaced by Slavic words. In some cases, standard Romanian also independently borrowed the same word.
</p>
<ul><li>Bulgarian (Slavic) <span title="Bulgarian-language text"><i lang="bg-Latn">drob</i></span> &gt; MR <b><span title="Megleno Romanian-language text"><i lang="ruq">drob</i></span></b></li>
<li>Bulgarian <span title="Bulgarian-language text"><i lang="bg-Latn">neviasta</i></span> &gt; MR <b><span title="Megleno Romanian-language text"><i lang="ruq">niveastă</i></span></b> (bride)</li>
<li>Bulgarian <span title="Bulgarian-language text"><i lang="bg-Latn">gora</i></span> &gt; MR <b><span title="Megleno Romanian-language text"><i lang="ruq">goră</i></span></b> (forest)</li></ul>
