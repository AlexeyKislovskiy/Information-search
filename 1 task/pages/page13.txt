<p>The <b>Sibe</b> language (<a class="mw-selflink selflink">Xibe</a>: <span lang="sjo"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1161532786"><span class="font-mong" style="display:inline-block; font-weight:normal; font-size: 1.25em; line-height: 1.2em; -webkit-writing-mode: vertical-lr; -o-writing-mode: vertical-lr; -ms-writing-mode: tb-lr; writing-mode: tb-lr; writing-mode: vertical-lr;; text-orientation: sideways; vertical-align:text-top;">ᠰᡳᠪᡝ ᡤᡳᠰᡠᠨ</span></span>, <small>romanized:&#160;</small><span title="Xibe-language romanization"><i lang="sjo-Latn">sibe gisun</i></span>, also <b>Sibo</b>, <b>Xibe</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Tungusic_languages" title="Tungusic languages">Tungusic language</a> spoken by members of the <a href="/wiki/Sibe_people" title="Sibe people">Sibe</a> minority of <a href="/wiki/Xinjiang" title="Xinjiang">Xinjiang</a>, in Northwest China.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Classification">Classification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xibe_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Classification"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sibe is conventionally viewed<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag may use weasel words or too-vague attribution. (November 2012)">by whom?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> as a separate language within the southern group of <a href="/wiki/Tungusic_languages" title="Tungusic languages">Tungusic languages</a> alongside the more well-known <a href="/wiki/Manchu_language" title="Manchu language">Manchu language</a>, having undergone more than 200 years of development separated from the Tungusic-speaking heartland since Sibe troops were dispatched to the Xinjiang frontiers in 1764. Some researchers such as <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Norman_(sinologist)" title="Jerry Norman (sinologist)">Jerry Norman</a> hold that Sibe is a dialect of Manchu, whereas Xibologists such as <a href="/w/index.php?title=An_Jun&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="An Jun (page does not exist)">An Jun</a> argue that Sibe should be considered the "successor" to Manchu. Ethnohistorically, the Sibe people are not considered <a href="/wiki/Manchu_people" title="Manchu people">Manchu people</a>, because they were excluded from chieftain <a href="/wiki/Nurhaci" title="Nurhaci">Nurhaci</a>'s 17th-century tribal confederation to which the name "Manchu" was later applied.<sup id="cite_ref-Gorelova_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gorelova-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Phonology">Phonology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xibe_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Phonology"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sibe is <a href="/wiki/Mutual_intelligibility" title="Mutual intelligibility">mutually intelligible</a> with Manchu,<sup id="cite_ref-Ethnologue_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ethnologue-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> although unlike Manchu, Sibe has reported to have eight vowel distinctions as opposed to the six found in Manchu, as well as differences in morphology, and a more complex system of <a href="/wiki/Vowel_harmony" title="Vowel harmony">vowel harmony</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Ramsey_1989_p=215_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ramsey_1989_p=215-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Morphology">Morphology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xibe_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Morphology"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sibe has seven case morphemes, three of which are used quite differently from modern Manchu. The categorization of morphemes as case markers in spoken Sibe is partially controversial due to the status of numerous suffixes in the language. Despite the general controversy about the categorization of case markers versus postpositions in Tungusic languages, four case markers in Sibe are shared with literary Manchu (Nominative, Genitive, Dative-Locative and Accusative). Sibe's three innovated cases – the ablative, lative, and instrumental-sociative share their meanings with similar case forms in neighboring Uyghur, Kazakh, and Oiryat Mongolian.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lexicon">Lexicon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xibe_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Lexicon"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The general vocabulary and structure of Sibe has not been affected as much by <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a> as Manchu has. However, Sibe has absorbed a large body of Chinese sociological terminology, especially in politics: like <i>gəming</i> ("revolution", from <span title="Chinese-language text"><span lang="zh">革命</span></span>) and <i>zhuxi</i> ("chairperson", from <span title="Chinese-language text"><span lang="zh">主席</span></span>),<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and economics: like <i>chūna</i> ("cashier", from <span title="Chinese-language text"><span lang="zh">出纳</span></span>) and <i>daikuan</i> ("loan", from <span title="Chinese-language text"><span lang="zh">贷款</span></span>). Written Sibe is more conservative and rejecting of loanwords, but spoken Sibe contains additional Chinese-derived vocabulary such as <i>nan</i> (from <span title="Chinese-language text"><span lang="zh">男</span></span>) for "man" where the Manchu-based equivalent is <i>niyalma</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Gorelova_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gorelova-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> There has also been some influence from <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> including words such as <i>konsul</i> ("consul", from <span title="Russian-language text"><span lang="ru">консул</span></span>) and <i>mashina</i> ("sewing machine", from <span title="Russian-language text"><span lang="ru">машина</span></span>).<sup id="cite_ref-Gorelova_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gorelova-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Smaller Xinjiang languages contribute mostly cultural terminology, such as <i>namas</i> ("an Islamic feast") from <a href="/wiki/Uygur_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Uygur language">Uygur</a> and <i>baige</i> ("horse race") from <a href="/wiki/Kazakh_language" title="Kazakh language">Kazakh</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Gorelova_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gorelova-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Writing_system">Writing system<span class="anchor" id="Xibe_alphabet"></span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xibe_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Writing system"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Manchu_alphabet" title="Manchu alphabet">Manchu alphabet</a></div>
<p>Sibe is written in a derivative of the <a href="/wiki/Manchu_alphabet" title="Manchu alphabet">Manchu alphabet</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Ramsey_1989_p=215_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ramsey_1989_p=215-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The Sibe alphabet diverges from the Manchu alphabet in that the positions of the letters in some words have changed, Sibe lacks 13 out of 131 syllables in Manchu, and Sibe has three syllables that are not found in Manchu (<i>wi</i>, <i>wo</i>, and <i>wu</i>).<sup id="cite_ref-Gorelova_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gorelova-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Usage">Usage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xibe_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Usage"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<figure class="mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:CabcalSerkin.svg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/CabcalSerkin.svg/200px-CabcalSerkin.svg.png" decoding="async" width="200" height="211" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/CabcalSerkin.svg/300px-CabcalSerkin.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/CabcalSerkin.svg/400px-CabcalSerkin.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="279" data-file-height="295" /></a><figcaption>"Cabcal Serkin" in Sibe script (the name of <a href="/wiki/Qapqal_News" title="Qapqal News">Qapqal News</a>, the world's only Sibe-language newspaper)</figcaption></figure>
<p>In 1998, there were eight <a href="/wiki/Primary_school" title="Primary school">primary schools</a> that taught Sibe in the <a href="/wiki/Qapqal_Xibe_Autonomous_County" title="Qapqal Xibe Autonomous County">Qapqal Xibe Autonomous County</a> where the medium of instruction was Chinese, but Sibe lessons were mandatory. From 1954 to 1959, the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Publishing_House" class="mw-redirect" title="People&#39;s Publishing House">People's Publishing House</a> in <a href="/wiki/%C3%9Cr%C3%BCmqi" title="Ürümqi">Ürümqi</a> published over 285 significant works, including government documents, <i><a href="/wiki/Belles-lettres" title="Belles-lettres">belles-lettres</a></i>, and schoolbooks, in Sibe.<sup id="cite_ref-Gorelova_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gorelova-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Since 1946, the Sibe-language <i><a href="/wiki/Qapqal_News" title="Qapqal News">Qapqal News</a></i> has been published in <a href="/wiki/Yining_City" class="mw-redirect" title="Yining City">Yining</a>. In Qapqal, Sibe-language programming is allocated 15 minutes per day of radio broadcasting and 15 to 30-minute television programmes broadcast once or twice per month.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Sibe is taught as a second language at the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ili_Normal_University&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ili Normal University (page does not exist)">Ili Normal University</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Ili_Kazakh_Autonomous_Prefecture" title="Ili Kazakh Autonomous Prefecture">Ili Kazakh Autonomous Prefecture</a> of northern <a href="/wiki/Xinjiang" title="Xinjiang">Xinjiang</a>; it established an <a href="/wiki/Academic_major" title="Academic major">undergraduate major</a> in the language in 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-YiliWanbao_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-YiliWanbao-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> A few <a href="/wiki/Manchu_language" title="Manchu language">Manchu language</a> enthusiasts from Eastern China have visited Qapqal Sibe County in order to experience an environment where a variety closely related to Manchu is spoken natively.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
