<p>The <b>Yeni language</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Extinct_language" title="Extinct language">extinct language</a> of <a href="/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a>, formerly spoken around Djeni Mountain in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nyalang&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nyalang (page does not exist)">Nyalang</a> area.  All that remains of the language, apparently, is a song remembered by some <a href="/wiki/Kwanja_language" title="Kwanja language">Sandani</a> speakers.  However, according to Bruce Connell (the first linguist to report its existence, in 1995), comparison of the song's words to neighboring languages suggests that "it was closely related to [the <a href="/wiki/Mambiloid_languages" title="Mambiloid languages">Mambiloid languages</a>] <a href="/wiki/Cambap_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Cambap language">Cambap</a>, <a href="/wiki/Njerep_language" title="Njerep language">Njerep</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kasabe_language" title="Kasabe language">Kasabe</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
