<p><b>Guinea-Bissau Creole</b>, also known as <b>Kiriol</b> or <b>Crioulo</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Creole_language" title="Creole language">creole language</a> whose lexicon <a href="/wiki/Portuguese-based_creole_languages" title="Portuguese-based creole languages">derives mostly from Portuguese</a>. It is spoken in <a href="/wiki/Guinea_Bissau" class="mw-redirect" title="Guinea Bissau">Guinea Bissau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">Senegal</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Gambia" title="The Gambia">The Gambia</a>. It is also called by its native speakers as <span title="Upper Guinea Crioulo-language text"><i lang="pov">guinensi</i></span>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <span title="Upper Guinea Crioulo-language text"><i lang="pov">kriyol</i></span>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> or <span title="Upper Guinea Crioulo-language text"><i lang="pov">portuguis</i></span>.
</p><p>Guinea-Bissau Creole is spoken as a native tongue by 250,000 Bissau-Guineans<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2024)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> and as a second language by 1,000,000.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2024)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>A variant of Guinea-Bissau Creole is also spoken in southern <a href="/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">Senegal</a>, mainly in the region of <a href="/wiki/Casamance" title="Casamance">Casamance</a>, a former Portuguese colony, which is known as Portuguis Creole or <a href="/wiki/Casamance_Creole" title="Casamance Creole">Casamance Creole</a>. Creole is the majority language of the inhabitants of the <a href="/wiki/Casamance" title="Casamance">Casamance</a> region and is used as a language of commerce.<sup id="cite_ref-instituto-camoes_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-instituto-camoes-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Standard <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a> is the official language of Guinea-Bissau, but Guinea-Bissau Creole is the language of trade, informal literature and entertainment. It is not used in either news media, parliament, public services or educational programming.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guinea-Bissau_Creole&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The creole languages of Upper Guinea are the oldest-known creoles whose lexicons derive heavily from Portuguese. They first appeared around Portuguese settlements established along the northwest coast of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>; Guinea-Bissau Creole was among these Portuguese-lexified creoles to have emerged. Portuguese merchants and settlers started to mix with locals almost immediately. A small body of settlers called <i><a href="/wiki/Lan%C3%A7ados" title="Lançados">lançados</a></i> ("the thrown out ones"), contributed to the spread of the Portuguese language and influence by being intermediaries between the Portuguese and natives.
</p><p>There are three main varieties of this creole in Guinea-Bissau and Senegal: Bissau and Bolama, Bafata, and Cacheu–Ziguinchor.
</p><p>The creole's substrate language is the language of the local peoples: Mandingas, <a href="/wiki/Manjacos" class="mw-redirect" title="Manjacos">Manjacos</a>, Pepéis and others, but most of the lexicon (around 80%) comes from Portuguese.
</p><p>The Portuguese-influenced dialect of <a href="/wiki/Casamance" title="Casamance">Casamance</a>, known as <a href="/wiki/Casamance_Creole" title="Casamance Creole">Portuguis Creole</a> or <a href="/wiki/Casamance_Creole" title="Casamance Creole">Casamance Creole</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> similar to the one of Cacheu (Guinea-Bissau) has some influence of <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>. <i><span title="Upper Guinea Crioulo-language text"><i lang="pov">Fijus di Terra</i></span></i> (<span title="Portuguese-language text"><i lang="pt">Filhos da Terra</i></span>, English: Children of the Land) and <i><span title="Upper Guinea Crioulo-language text"><i lang="pov">Fijus di Fidalgu</i></span></i> (Port. <i><span title="Portuguese-language text"><i lang="pt">Filhos de Fidalgo</i></span></i>, Eng. Children of Nobles) speak it, all of them are known, locally, as Purtuguis because they adopt <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> habits, are <a href="/wiki/Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Catholic">Catholics</a> and speak a Creole. They are descendants of Portuguese men and African women. Most of them have Portuguese surnames, such as <i>da Silva</i>, <i>Carvalho</i> or <i>Fonseca</i>. The former Casamance Kingdom made a friendly alliance with the Portuguese and the local king adopted European lifestyle, and there were Portuguese in his court. In 1899, the city was ceded to <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> and in the middle of the 20th century, the language spread to the surrounding area. After Senegal's independence from France, the Creole people were seen as friends of the French, and discrimination by the more numerous northern <a href="/wiki/Wolof_language" title="Wolof language">Wolof</a>-speaking community started, which has caused Casamance to struggle for independence since 1982. Today, although they continue to struggle, the movement is more placid and learning Portuguese is popular in Casamance because they see it has a link to their past. It is also learned across Senegal since the independence of the country from France.<sup id="cite_ref-instituto-camoes_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-instituto-camoes-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In Senegal, the creole is the first language of at least 46,500 people (1998); it is mainly spoken in Ziguinchor, but there are also speakers in other Casamance cities and in <a href="/wiki/The_Gambia" title="The Gambia">The Gambia</a>.
</p><p>The use of Guinea-Bissau Creole is still expanding<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> but with growing interference from Portuguese (due to television, literacy, prestige and emigration to Portugal) and African languages (through the migration of speakers of native African languages to the main urban centres of Guinea-Bissau, where the creole is prevalent).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Example">Example</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guinea-Bissau_Creole&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Example"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Universal Declaration of Human Rights</b>
</p><p><i>Creole:</i> <span title="Upper Guinea Crioulo-language text"><span lang="pov" style="font-style: normal;">Tudu pekaduris ta padidu libri i igual na balur suma na diritus. Suma e dadu kapasidadi di pensa, e tene tambi konsiensia, e dibi di trata nutru suma ermons.</span></span><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Portuguese:</i>  os seres humanos nascem livres e iguais em dignidade e em direitos. Dotados de razão e de consciência, devem agir uns para com os outros em espírito de fraternidade.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>English</i>: All human beings are born free and equal in dignity and rights. They are endowed with reason and conscience and should act towards one another in a spirit of brotherhood.
</p>
