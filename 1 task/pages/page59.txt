<p><b>Saharan Spanish</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">español saharaui</i>) is the <a href="/wiki/Varieties_of_Spanish" class="mw-redirect" title="Varieties of Spanish">variety</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish language</a> spoken in <a href="/wiki/Western_Sahara" title="Western Sahara">Western Sahara</a> and adjacent regions. This non-native variety is heavily influenced by both Spanish cultural links and a strong expatriate community who live in <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hispanic_America" title="Hispanic America">Hispanic America</a>, particularly <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>.

<p>Although the native and dominant languages in <a href="/wiki/Western_Sahara" title="Western Sahara">Western Sahara</a> are <a href="/wiki/Hassaniya" class="mw-redirect" title="Hassaniya">Hassaniya</a> <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a> and some <a href="/wiki/Berber_languages" title="Berber languages">Berber languages</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> was introduced by settlers in <a href="/wiki/Spanish_West_Africa" title="Spanish West Africa">Spanish West Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Sahara" title="Spanish Sahara">Spanish Sahara</a> in the 19th century. Older Sahrawis who went to school in the time of the Spanish colonization (up to 1975) are typically competent in the language, and in addition Spanish is taught to the new generations in the <a href="/wiki/Sahrawi_refugee_camps" title="Sahrawi refugee camps">Sahrawi refugee camps</a> near <a href="/wiki/Tindouf" title="Tindouf">Tindouf</a>, Algeria. In the Moroccan-ruled parts of the country, the foreign language taught in school is typically French, rather than Spanish.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Current_usage_and_legal_status">Current usage and legal status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saharan_Spanish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Current usage and legal status"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Spanish still influences Sahrawi society today and is the preferred <a href="/wiki/Second_language" title="Second language">second language</a> for acquisition and government in the <a href="/wiki/Sahrawi_Arab_Democratic_Republic" title="Sahrawi Arab Democratic Republic">Sahrawi Arab Democratic Republic</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-ident_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ident-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> though not in the Moroccan-ruled areas that make up most of the territory. Arabic is the sole official language identified in the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_the_Sahrawi_Arab_Democratic_Republic" title="Constitution of the Sahrawi Arab Democratic Republic">Sahrawi constitution</a>, and the republic only uses Spanish for radio and TV broadcasts<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and state journalism.<sup id="cite_ref-sps_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sps-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Cervantes_Institute" class="mw-redirect" title="Cervantes Institute">Cervantes Institute</a> estimates that there are 22,000 second-language speakers, 5% of the population, in Western Sahara, plus a larger number in the <a href="/wiki/Sahrawi_refugee_camps" title="Sahrawi refugee camps">refugee camps in Algeria</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ci_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ci-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Spanish <a href="/wiki/Vocabulary" title="Vocabulary">vocabulary</a> has entered Hassaniya, particularly in fields related to agriculture, automobiles, diet, and sanitation.<sup id="cite_ref-boletin_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-boletin-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> These loanwords are reinforced due to Sahrawis studying abroad in Hispanic lands and returning to either Western Sahara or the Sahrawi refugee camps.<sup id="cite_ref-boletin_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-boletin-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
