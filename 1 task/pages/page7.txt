<p><i><b>Nenqayni Chʼih</b></i> (lit. "the Native way") (also <b>Chilcotin</b>, <b>Tŝilhqotʼin</b>, <b>Tsilhqotʼin</b>, <b>Tsilhqútʼin</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Athabaskan_languages#Northern_Athabaskan" title="Athabaskan languages">Northern Athabaskan language</a> spoken in <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Tsilhqot%CA%BCin" title="Tsilhqotʼin">Tsilhqotʼin</a> people.
</p><p>The name <i>Chilcotin</i> is derived from the Chilcotin name for themselves: <span title="Chilcotin-language text"><i lang="clc">Tŝilhqotʼin</i></span> literally "people of the red ochre river".
</p>

<ul><li>Like many other <a href="/wiki/Athabaskan_languages" title="Athabaskan languages">Athabaskan languages</a>, Chilcotin does not have a contrast between <a href="/wiki/Fricative" title="Fricative">fricatives</a> and <a href="/wiki/Approximant" title="Approximant">approximants</a>.</li>
<li>The alveolar series is <a href="/wiki/Pharyngealization" title="Pharyngealization">pharyngealized</a>.</li>
<li>Dentals and alveolars:
<ul><li>Both Krauss (1975) and Cook (1993) describe the dental and alveolar as being essentially identical in articulation, <i>postdental</i>, with the only differentiating factor being their different behaviours in the vowel flattening processes (described below).</li>
<li>Gafos (1999, personal communication with Cook) describes the dental series as <i>apico-laminal denti-alveolar</i> and the alveolar series as <i>lamino-postalveolar</i>.</li></ul></li></ul>

<td><span class="IPA" lang="und-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Near-open_front_unrounded_vowel" title="Near-open front unrounded vowel">æ</a></span> <span class="nowrap">&#x27e8;a&#x27e9;</span>
</td>
<td><span class="IPA" lang="und-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Open-mid_front_unrounded_vowel" title="Open-mid front unrounded vowel">ɛ</a></span> <span class="nowrap">&#x27e8;e&#x27e9;</span>

<ul><li>Chilcotin has both tense and lax vowel <a href="/wiki/Phoneme" title="Phoneme">phonemes</a>. Additionally, tense vowels may become lax from vowel laxing.</li></ul>
<p>Every given Chilcotin vowel has a number of different phonetic realizations from complex <a href="/wiki/Phonology" title="Phonology">phonological</a> processes (such as nasalization, laxing, flattening). For instance, the vowel <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/i/</span> can be variously pronounced <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[i,<span class="wrap"> </span>ĩ,<span class="wrap"> </span>ɪ,<span class="wrap"> </span>e,<span class="wrap"> </span>ᵊi,<span class="wrap"> </span>ᵊĩ,<span class="wrap"> </span>ᵊɪ]</span>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tone">Tone</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tsilhqot%CA%BCin_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Tone"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Chilcotin is a <a href="/wiki/Tonal_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Tonal language">tonal language</a> with two tones: high tone and low tone.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Phonological_processes">Phonological processes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tsilhqot%CA%BCin_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Phonological processes"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Chilcotin has vowel flattening and consonant harmony. <a href="/wiki/Consonant_harmony" title="Consonant harmony">Consonant harmony</a> (<a href="/wiki/Sibilant" title="Sibilant">sibilant</a> harmony) is rather common in the Athabaskan language family. Vowel flattening is unique to Chilcotin but is similar to phonological processes in other unrelated Interior <a href="/wiki/Salishan_languages" title="Salishan languages">Salishan languages</a> spoken in the same area, such as <a href="/wiki/Shuswap_language" title="Shuswap language">Shuswap</a>, <a href="/wiki/St%CA%BCat%CA%BCimcets_language#Phonological_processes" class="mw-redirect" title="Stʼatʼimcets language">Stʼátʼimcets</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Thompson_River_Salish" class="mw-redirect" title="Thompson River Salish">Thompson River Salish</a> (and thus was probably borrowed into Chilcotin). That type of harmony is an <a href="/wiki/Areal_feature_(linguistics)" class="mw-redirect" title="Areal feature (linguistics)">areal feature</a> common in this region of North America. The Chilcotin processes, however, are much more complicated.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Vowel_nasalization_and_laxing">Vowel nasalization and laxing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tsilhqot%CA%BCin_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Vowel nasalization and laxing"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Vowel nasalization is a phonological process by which the phoneme <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/n/</span> is nasalizes the preceding vowel. It occurs when the vowel + <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/n/</span> sequence is followed by a (tautosyllabic) <a href="/wiki/Continuant" title="Continuant">continuant</a> consonant (such as <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/ɬ,<span class="wrap"> </span>sˤ,<span class="wrap"> </span>zˤ,<span class="wrap"> </span>ç,<span class="wrap"> </span>j,<span class="wrap"> </span>χ/</span>).
</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Vowel_flattening">Vowel flattening</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tsilhqot%CA%BCin_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Vowel flattening"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Chilcotin has a type of <a href="/wiki/Vowel#Tongue_root_position" title="Vowel">retracted tongue root</a> <a href="/wiki/Vowel_harmony" title="Vowel harmony">harmony</a>. Generally, "flat" consonants lower vowels in both directions. <a href="/wiki/Assimilation_(linguistics)" class="mw-redirect" title="Assimilation (linguistics)">Assimilation</a> is both progressive and regressive.
</p><p>Chilcotin consonants can be grouped into three categories: neutral, sharp, and flat.

<ul><li>Flat consonants trigger vowel flattening.</li>
<li>Sharp consonants block vowel flattening.</li>
<li>Neutral consonants do not affect vowel flattening in any way.</li></ul>
<p>The flat consonants can be further divided into two types:
</p>
<ol><li>a <b><span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">sˤ</span></b>-series (i.e. <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/tsˤ,<span class="wrap"> </span>tsʰˤ,<span class="wrap"> </span>tsʼˤ/ˌ</span> etc.), and</li>
<li>a <b><span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">q</span></b>-series (i.e. <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/q,<span class="wrap"> </span>qʷ,<span class="wrap"> </span>qʰ/ˌ</span> etc.).</li></ol>
<p>The <b><span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">sˤ</span></b>-series is stronger than the <b><span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">q</span></b>-series by affecting vowels farther away.

<p>The progressive and regressive flattening processes are described below.
</p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Progressive_flattening">Progressive flattening</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tsilhqot%CA%BCin_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Progressive flattening"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>In the <i>progressive</i> (left-to-right) flattening, the <b><span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">q</span></b>-series consonants affect only the immediately following vowel:
</p>
<dl><dd><table>
<tbody><tr>
<td>/ʁitʰi/</td>
<td>→</td>
<td>[ʁᵊitʰi]
</td>
<td>&#160;
</td>
<td>'I slept'
</td>
<td>&#160;
</td>
<td><small>(ʁ flattens i → ᵊi)</small>
</td></tr>
<tr>
<td>/qʰænɪç/</td>
<td>→</td>
<td>[qʰanɪç]
</td>
<td>&#160;
</td>
<td>'spoon'
</td>
<td>&#160;
</td>
<td><small>(qʰ flattens æ → a)</small>
</td></tr></tbody></table></dd></dl>
<p>Like the <b><span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">q</span></b>-series, the stronger <b><span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">sˤ</span></b>-series consonants affects the immediately following vowel. However, it affects the vowel in the following <a href="/wiki/Syllable" title="Syllable">syllable</a> as well if the first flattened vowel is a lax vowel. If the first flattened is tense, the vowel of the following syllable is not flattened.
</p>
<dl><dd><table>
<tbody><tr>

<p>Thus, the neutral consonants are transparent in the flattening process. In the first word <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/sˤɛɬ.tʰin/</span> 'he's comatose', <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/sˤ/</span> flattens the <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/ɛ/</span> of the first syllable to <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[ə]</span> and the <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/i/</span> of the second syllable to <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[ᵊi]</span>. In the word <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/sˤi.tʰin/</span> 'I'm sleeping', <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/sˤ/</span> flattens <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/i/</span> to <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[ᵊi]</span>. Since, however, the vowel of the first syllable is <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/i/</span>, which is a tense vowel, the <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/sˤ/</span> cannot flatten the <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/i/</span> of the second syllable.
</p><p>The sharp consonants, however, block the progressive flattening caused by the <b><span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">sˤ</span></b>-series:

<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Regressive_flattening">Regressive flattening</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tsilhqot%CA%BCin_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Regressive flattening"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
