<p><b>Korandje</b> (Korandje: <i>kwạṛa n dzyəy</i>; <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span lang="ar" dir="rtl">البلبالية</span>, <small><a href="/wiki/Romanization_of_Arabic" title="Romanization of Arabic">romanized</a>:&#160;</small><span title="Arabic-language romanization"><i lang="ar-Latn">al-Balbaliyyah</i></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Northern_Songhay_languages" title="Northern Songhay languages">Northern Songhay language</a><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> which is by far the most northerly of the <a href="/wiki/Songhay_languages" title="Songhay languages">Songhay languages</a>. It is spoken around the Algerian oasis of <a href="/wiki/Tabelbala" title="Tabelbala">Tabelbala</a> by about 3,000 people; its name literally means "village's language". While retaining a basically Songhay structure, it is extremely heavily influenced by <a href="/wiki/Berber_languages" title="Berber languages">Berber</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arabic" title="Arabic">Arabic</a>; about 20% of the 100-word <a href="/wiki/Swadesh_list" title="Swadesh list">Swadesh list</a> of basic vocabulary consists of loanwords from Arabic or Berber, and the proportion of the lexicon as a whole is considerably higher.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The only published studies of Korandje based on first-hand data are Cancel (1908),<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> a 45-page article by a French lieutenant covering basic grammar and vocabulary and a couple of sample texts; Champault (1969),<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> an anthropological study containing some incidental linguistically relevant materials such as sentences and rhymes; Tilmatine (1991, 1996),<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> an article (published in German, then reworked in French) revisiting Cancel and Champault and adding about a page of new data recorded by the author; and Souag (2010a, 2010b),<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> the former arguing the case for <a href="/wiki/Western_Berber" class="mw-redirect" title="Western Berber">Western Berber</a> loans in the lexicon, the latter studying the effect of contact with Berber and Arabic on its grammar.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Phonology">Phonology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Korandje_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Phonology"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Grammar">Grammar</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Korandje_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Grammar"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pronouns">Pronouns</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Korandje_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Pronouns"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The pronouns are: <i>aγəy</i>, I; <i>ni</i>, you; <i>ana</i>, he/she/it; <i>yayu</i>, we; <i>ndzyu</i>, you (plural); <i>ini</i>, they. Possessive forms are <i>ʕan</i>, my; <i>nən</i>, your; <i>an</i>, his/her/its; <i>yan</i>, our; <i>ndzən</i>, your (pl.); <i>in</i>, their. Subject agreement prefixes on the verb are <i>ʕa-</i> I; <i>n-</i>, you; <i>a-</i>, he/she/it; <i>ya-</i>, we; <i>ndz-</i>, you (plural); <i>i-</i>, they.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Verbs">Verbs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Korandje_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Verbs"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>According to Tilmatine, verbs are negated by surrounding them with <i>`as ... hé/hi</i>, e.g. <i>ni `as ba enγa hé</i> &gt; <i>n`esbanγa hé</i> "do not eat!". "No" is <i>hoho</i> or <i>ho</i>: <i>n'd'xani bînu, willa ho?</i> "did you sleep yesterday, or not?".
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nouns">Nouns</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Korandje_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Nouns"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The most productive plural marker is the <a href="/wiki/Clitic" title="Clitic">clitic</a> <i>=yu</i>:
</p>
<div class="interlinear" style="margin-left:4em"><div style="position:absolute;left:1em">e.g.</div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">tsəksi</p><p style="margin: 0px;">"goat"</p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">&gt;</p><p style="margin: 0px;">&#160;</p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">tsəks=yu</p><p style="margin: 0px;">"goats"</p></div><p style="display: none;">tsəksi &gt; tsəks=yu</p><p style="display: none;">"goat" {} "goats"</p><p class="mw-empty-elt"></p><div style="clear: left; display: block;"></div>
</div>
<p>This marker comes at the end of the "core noun phrase", the unit consisting of noun+numeral+adjective+demonstrative:
</p>
<div class="interlinear" style="margin-left:4em"><div style="position:absolute;left:1em">e.g.</div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">ạḍṛạ</p><p style="margin: 0px;">mountain</p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">inẓa</p><p style="margin: 0px;">three</p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">bya=γ=yu</p><p style="margin: 0px;">big=<abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="demonstrative" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">DEM</abbr>=<abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="plural number" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">PL</abbr></p></div><p style="display: none;">ạḍṛạ inẓa bya=γ=yu</p><p style="display: none;">mountain three big=DEM=PL</p><p style="clear: left;">"these three big mountains"<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup></p><div style="clear: left; display: block;"></div>
</div>
<p>Some Berber loans retain versions of their original plurals, usually with the <a href="/wiki/Circumfix" title="Circumfix">circumfix</a> <i>(ts)i-...-ən</i>,
</p>
<div class="interlinear" style="margin-left:4em"><div style="position:absolute;left:1em">e.g.</div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">awṛəẓ</p><p style="margin: 0px;">"heel"</p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">&gt;</p><p style="margin: 0px;">&#160;</p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">iwṛạẓən</p><p style="margin: 0px;">"heels"</p></div><p style="display: none;">awṛəẓ &gt; iwṛạẓən</p><p style="display: none;">"heel" {} "heels"</p><p class="mw-empty-elt"></p><div style="clear: left; display: block;"></div>
</div>
<p>While the morphemes involved are clearly of Berber origin, the details of this system differ from any one attested Berber language, and this plural is extended to at least one item of Songhay origin, <i>tsạṛə̣w</i> "spoon" &gt; <i>tsiṛạwən</i>. Some Arabic loans similarly retain Arabic plurals.
</p><p>The possessive is expressed by the particle <i>n</i>, with the possessor preceding the possessed:
</p>
<div class="interlinear" style="margin-left:4em"><div style="position:absolute;left:1em">e.g.</div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">wi</p><p style="margin: 0px;">woman</p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">n</p><p style="margin: 0px;"><abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="possessive marker" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">POSS</abbr></p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">tsə̣ffạ</p><p style="margin: 0px;">knife</p></div><p style="display: none;">wi n tsə̣ffạ</p><p style="display: none;">woman POSS knife</p><p style="clear: left;">"woman's knife"</p><div style="clear: left; display: block;"></div>
</div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Numbers">Numbers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Korandje_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Numbers"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The only non-Arabic numbers in normal use are <i>a-ffu</i> "one", <i>inka</i> "two", and <i>inẓa</i> "three". There also exist "cryptic" (<a href="/wiki/Argot" class="mw-redirect" title="Argot">argot</a>) and children's counting systems. The syntax of numerals in the noun phrases is complicated.
</p>
