					<div id="mw-content-text" class="mw-body-content"><div class="mw-content-ltr mw-parser-output" lang="en" dir="ltr"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Pronunciation system for Hebrew used by Italian Jews</div>
<p><b>Italian Hebrew</b> or <b>Italki Hebrew</b> refers to the pronunciation system for <a href="/wiki/Liturgical_Hebrew" class="mw-redirect" title="Liturgical Hebrew">liturgical Hebrew</a> traditionally used by <a href="/wiki/Italian_Jews" title="Italian Jews">Italian Jews</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Features">Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Italian_Hebrew&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Features"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Italian pronunciation of <a href="/wiki/Hebrew_language" title="Hebrew language">Hebrew</a> is similar to that of conservative <a href="/wiki/Spanish_and_Portuguese_Jews" title="Spanish and Portuguese Jews">Spanish and Portuguese Jews</a>.  Distinguishing features are:
</p>
<ul><li><i><a href="/wiki/Bet_(letter)" title="Bet (letter)">beth</a> <a href="/wiki/Rafe" title="Rafe">rafe</a></i> is pronounced <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[v]</span>;</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/He_(letter)" title="He (letter)">he</a></i> is often silent, as in the family name "Coen";</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Waw_(letter)" title="Waw (letter)">vav</a></i> is normally <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[v]</span> as in most Hebrew dialects, but can become <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[w]</span> in diphthongs (as in the family name "Anau").  Thus, in construct masculine plurals with male singular possessive suffix יו-, the pronunciation is not [-<span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">av]</span> but [-<span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">au]</span>;</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Zayin" title="Zayin">zayin</a></i> is often pronounced <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[dz]</span> like Italian voiced "z";</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Ayin" title="Ayin">ayin</a></i> is pronounced <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[ŋ]</span> (like English "ng" in "sing").  In some dialects, like the Roman, this sometimes becomes <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[ɲ]</span>, like the Italian combination "gn";</li>
<li>final <i><a href="/wiki/Taw" title="Taw">tav</a></i> is pronounced <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[d]</span>;</li>
<li>speakers in communities south of the <a href="/wiki/La_Spezia%E2%80%93Rimini_Line" title="La Spezia–Rimini Line">La Spezia</a> <a href="/wiki/Isogloss" title="Isogloss">isogloss</a>, and Jewish communities transplanted north of this, pronounce <i>dagesh forte</i> as a true geminate sound, in keeping with the pronunciation of double letters in Italian.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<p>This pronunciation has in many cases been adopted by the Sephardi, Ashkenazi and <i>Appam</i> communities of Italy as well as by the <a href="/wiki/Italian_Nusach" title="Italian Nusach">Italian rite</a> communities.
</p>
