<p>The <b>Munji</b> language (<a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>: <span lang="fa" dir="rtl">مونجی</span>), also known as <b>Munjani</b> (<span title="Persian-language text"><span lang="fa" dir="rtl">مونجانی</span></span>), <b>Munjhan</b> (<span title="Persian-language text"><span lang="fa" dir="rtl">مونجهان</span></span>), and the <b>Munjiwar</b> language,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Pamir_languages" title="Pamir languages">Pamir language</a> spoken in the Munjan valley in the <a href="/wiki/Kuran_wa_Munjan_District" title="Kuran wa Munjan District">Kuran wa Munjan district</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Badakhshan_Province" title="Badakhshan Province">Badakhshan province</a> in northeast <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>. It is similar to the <a href="/wiki/Yidgha_language" title="Yidgha language">Yidgha language</a>, which is spoken in the Upper <a href="/wiki/Lotkoh" title="Lotkoh">Lotkoh</a> Valley of <a href="/wiki/Chitral_District" title="Chitral District">Chitral</a>, west of <a href="/wiki/Garam_Chashma" title="Garam Chashma">Garam Chashma</a> in <a href="/wiki/Khyber_Pakhtunkhwa" title="Khyber Pakhtunkhwa">Khyber Pakhtunkhwa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Historically, Munji displays the closest possible linguistic affinity with the now-extinct <a href="/wiki/Bactrian_language" title="Bactrian language">Bactrian language</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Garam Chashma area became important during the <a href="/wiki/Soviet%E2%80%93Afghan_War" title="Soviet–Afghan War">Soviet–Afghan War</a>. During the invasion, the Soviets were unable to stop the flow of arms and men back and forth across the <a href="/wiki/Dorah_Pass" title="Dorah Pass">Dorah Pass</a> that separates <a href="/wiki/Chitral" title="Chitral">Chitral</a>, in Pakistan, from <a href="/wiki/Badakshan" class="mw-redirect" title="Badakshan">Badakshan</a> in <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>. The two dialects spoken in the area of Mamalgha Valley and the area of Munjan Valley differed, being the northern and southern dialects. The language has moved to parts of Chitral, after the <a href="/wiki/War_in_Afghanistan_(2001%E2%80%93present)" class="mw-redirect" title="War in Afghanistan (2001–present)">War in Afghanistan</a> forced the Munji-speaking people to flee to safer areas.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Despite <a href="/wiki/Dari" title="Dari">Dari</a> being the predominant language of the region, attitudes towards Munji are highly positive, and among speakers, few predict a decline in use.<sup id="cite_ref-Beyer_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beyer-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
