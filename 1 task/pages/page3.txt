<p><b>Cocopah</b> is a Delta language of the <a href="/wiki/Yuman_languages" class="mw-redirect" title="Yuman languages">Yuman</a> language family spoken by the <a href="/wiki/Cocopah_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Cocopah people">Cocopah</a>. Cocopah is believed to have derived from the <a href="/wiki/Hokan_languages" title="Hokan languages">Hokan language</a>, and it is related to the other Native American languages of <a href="/wiki/Mojave_language" title="Mojave language">Mojave</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kumeyaay_language" title="Kumeyaay language">Kumeyaay</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Cocopah is considered an endangered language, with fewer than 400 speakers at the turn of the 21st century. However, in an effort to keep the language alive, <a href="/wiki/Yuma_County,_Arizona" title="Yuma County, Arizona">Yuma County's</a> Cocopah Museum began offering classes teaching Cocopah to children in 1998.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cocopah_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Much of the Cocopah language was passed down through speaking, rather than through writing. This, in large part, is due to the fact that the language did not have an alphabet for the majority of its existence. It was not until the 1970s that a written language was developed, when a scholar decided to approach this task for a dissertation. Although the creation of an alphabet was useful, the original proved to be less than ideal, and so a new one was developed by the tribe in the early 2000s. As the revival of the language progressed, it became apparent that the language did not have words to fit the advances made in modern society. In turn, the tribe developed new words to attribute to modern objects that did not exist in the ancient language. The elders of the tribe were given the responsibility of developing these new words and/or phrases. <sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>While the Cocopah tribe inhabits parts of Arizona and parts of Mexico, the written language differs based on the location of the tribe. For instance, Cocopah in Mexico use a different orthography than Cocopah in Arizona. The Mexican-based Cocopah use an orthography that was designed by the <a href="/wiki/INALI" class="mw-redirect" title="INALI">INALI</a>, an organization that examines and protects the rights of endangered languages.
</p>

<h3><span id="Syllable_.26_phonotactics"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Syllable_&amp;_phonotactics">Syllable &amp; phonotactics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cocopah_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Syllable &amp; phonotactics"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Cocopah <a href="/wiki/Syllable" title="Syllable">syllable</a>:
</p>
<dl><dd><span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">(C)(C)(C)V(ː)(C)(C)</span></dd></dl>
<ul><li>Word-initial two-consonant clusters usually consist of a fricative plus another consonant, e.g. <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/sp,<span class="wrap"> </span>ʂm,<span class="wrap"> </span>ʃp,<span class="wrap"> </span>xt͡ʃ/</span>. Rarer two-consonant clusters start with a lateral or a stop consonant, e.g. <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/lt͡ʃ,<span class="wrap"> </span>ɬʲt͡ʃ,<span class="wrap"> </span>ps,<span class="wrap"> </span>t͡ʃp/</span>.</li>
<li>Three-consonant clusters are rare, recorded examples include <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/pxk,<span class="wrap"> </span>pxkʷ,<span class="wrap"> </span>spx/</span>.</li></ul>
