<p><b>Torwali</b> (<span class="Nastaliq" dir="rtl" title="Nastaliq" style="font-family: &#39;Jameel Noori Nastaleeq&#39;, &#39;Urdu Typesetting&#39;, &#39;Noto Nastaliq Urdu&#39;, &#39;Noto Nastaliq Urdu Draft&#39;, &#39;Hussaini Nastaleeq&#39;, &#39;AlQalam Taj Nastaleeq&#39;, IranNastaliq, &#39;Awami Nastaliq&#39;, &#39;Awami Nastaliq Beta3&#39;, &#39;Awami Nastaliq Beta2&#39;, &#39;Awami Nastaliq Beta1&#39;, &#39;Nafees Nastaleeq&#39;, &#39;Nafees Nastaleeq v1.01&#39;, &#39;Pak Nastaleeq&#39;, &#39;PDMS_Jauhar&#39;, &#39;Alvi Lahori Nastaleeq&#39;; font-size: 110%; font-style: normal;">توروالی</span>) is an <a href="/wiki/Indo-Aryan_languages" title="Indo-Aryan languages">Indo-Aryan language</a> spoken  by the Torwali people, and concentrated in  the <a href="/wiki/Bahrain,_Pakistan" title="Bahrain, Pakistan">Bahrain</a> and Chail areas of the <a href="/wiki/Swat_District" title="Swat District">Swat District</a> in <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The Torwali language is said to have originated from the pre-Muslim communities of Swat.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> It is the closest modern <a href="/wiki/Indo-Aryan_languages" title="Indo-Aryan languages">Indo-Aryan language</a> still spoken today to <i>Niya</i>, a dialect of <a href="/wiki/Gandhari_language" title="Gandhari language">Gāndhārī</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Middle_Indo-Aryan_languages" title="Middle Indo-Aryan languages">Middle Indo-Aryan language</a> spoken in the ancient region of <a href="/wiki/Gandhara" title="Gandhara">Gandhara</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Torwali is an <a href="/wiki/Endangered_language" title="Endangered language">endangered language</a>: it is characterised as "definitely endangered" by <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a>'s Atlas of Endangered Languages,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and as "vulnerable" by the <a href="/wiki/Catalogue_of_Endangered_Languages" title="Catalogue of Endangered Languages">Catalogue of Endangered Languages</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> There have been efforts to revitalize the language since 2004, and mother tongue community schools have been established by Idara Baraye Taleem-o-Taraqi (IBT).<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chitrali_and_Kohistani_Alphabet">Chitrali and Kohistani Alphabet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Torwali_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Chitrali and Kohistani Alphabet"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Torwali language in <a href="/wiki/Chitral_district" class="mw-redirect" title="Chitral district">Chitral</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kohistan_District,_Pakistan" title="Kohistan District, Pakistan">Kohistan</a> uses loanwords from <a href="/wiki/Khowar_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Khowar language">Khowar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Indus_Kohistani" title="Indus Kohistani">Indus Kohistani</a>, the dominant languages in the region, which uses another letter, <a href="/w/index.php?title=%DD%B2&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="ݲ (page does not exist)">ݲ</a>, which is used to represent <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[ɖ͡ʐ]</span>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> They also use the <a href="/wiki/Pashto" title="Pashto">Pashto</a> letter <a href="/wiki/%DA%81" class="mw-redirect" title="ځ">ځ</a> to represent <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[d͡z]</span>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
