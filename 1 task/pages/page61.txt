<p>The <b>Mnong</b> language (also known as <b>Pnong</b> or <b>Bunong</b>) (Bunong: ឞូន៝ង) belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Austroasiatic_languages" title="Austroasiatic languages">Austro-Asiatic</a> language family.  It is spoken by the different groups of <a href="/wiki/Mnong_people" title="Mnong people">Mnong</a> in <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Pnong_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Pnong people">Pnong</a> group in <a href="/wiki/Cambodia" title="Cambodia">Cambodia</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mnong_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Distribution"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In Vietnam, Mnong is spoken in the districts of <a href="/wiki/%C4%90%C4%83k_Song_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Đăk Song District">Đăk Song</a>, <a href="/wiki/%C4%90%C4%83k_Mil_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Đăk Mil District">Đăk Mil</a>, <a href="/wiki/%C4%90%C4%83k_R%27L%E1%BA%A5p_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Đăk R&#39;Lấp District">Đăk R'Lấp</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kr%C3%B4ng_N%C3%B4_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Krông Nô District">Krông Nô</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gia_Ngh%C4%A9a" title="Gia Nghĩa">Gia Nghĩa</a>, and other nearby locations in <a href="/wiki/%C4%90%E1%BA%AFk_N%C3%B4ng_Province" class="mw-redirect" title="Đắk Nông Province">Đắk Nông Province</a> (Nguyễn &amp; Trương 2009).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Varieties">Varieties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mnong_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Varieties"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to <a href="/wiki/Ethnologue" title="Ethnologue">Ethnologue</a>, four major dialects exist: Central, Eastern and Southern Mnong (all spoken in Vietnam), and Kraol (spoken in Cambodia). Within a dialect group, members <a href="/wiki/Mutual_intelligibility" title="Mutual intelligibility">do not understand other dialects</a>. The Mnong language was studied first by the linguist Richard Phillips in the early 1970s.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Lê, et al. (2014:234-235)<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> lists the following subgroups of Mnong and their respective locations.
</p>
<ul><li><b>Mnông Gar</b>: in northwestern <a href="/wiki/L%C3%A2m_%C4%90%E1%BB%93ng_Province" class="mw-redirect" title="Lâm Đồng Province">Lâm Đồng Province</a> and southern <a href="/wiki/Lak_Lake" title="Lak Lake">Lak Lake</a>.</li>
<li><b>Mnông Nong</b>: in <a href="/wiki/%C4%90%E1%BA%AFk_N%C3%B4ng_Province" class="mw-redirect" title="Đắk Nông Province">Đắk Nông District</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=%C4%90%E1%BA%AFk_Min_District&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Đắk Min District (page does not exist)">Đắk Min District</a></li>
<li><b>Mnông Kuênh</b>: in <a href="/wiki/Kr%C3%B4ng_P%E1%BA%AFk_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Krông Pắk District">Krông Pắk District</a></li>
<li><b>Mnông Pré</b>: mainly in <a href="/wiki/%C4%90%E1%BA%AFk_N%C3%B4ng_Province" class="mw-redirect" title="Đắk Nông Province">Đắk Nông District</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=%C4%90%E1%BA%AFk_Min_District&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Đắk Min District (page does not exist)">Đắk Min District</a>, and a few at <a href="/wiki/Lak_Lake" title="Lak Lake">Lak Lake</a>.</li>
<li><b>Mnông Prâng</b>: scattered in <a href="/wiki/%C4%90%E1%BA%AFk_N%C3%B4ng_Province" class="mw-redirect" title="Đắk Nông Province">Đắk Nông District</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=%C4%90%E1%BA%AFk_Min_District&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Đắk Min District (page does not exist)">Đắk Min District</a>, and a few in southern <a href="/wiki/Lak_Lake" title="Lak Lake">Lak Lake</a> and in Bản Đon, <a href="/wiki/Ea_S%C3%BAp_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Ea Súp District">Ea Súp District</a>.</li>
<li><b>Mnông Rlăm</b>: in <a href="/wiki/L%E1%BA%AFk_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Lắk District">Lắk District</a>. Many have close relationships with the Ê-đê people.</li>
<li><b>Mnông Bu-đâng</b>: in Bản Đon, <a href="/wiki/Ea_S%C3%BAp_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Ea Súp District">Ea Súp District</a></li>
<li><b>Mnông Chỉl</b>: in <a href="/wiki/L%E1%BA%AFk_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Lắk District">Lắk District</a>. Many have close relationships with the Ê-đê people. Some also live in <a href="/wiki/L%E1%BA%A1c_D%C6%B0%C6%A1ng_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Lạc Dương District">Lạc Dương District</a> and <a href="/wiki/%C4%90%E1%BB%A9c_Tr%E1%BB%8Dng_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Đức Trọng District">Đức Trọng District</a> of <a href="/wiki/L%C3%A2m_%C4%90%E1%BB%93ng_Province" class="mw-redirect" title="Lâm Đồng Province">Lâm Đồng Province</a>.</li>
<li><b>Mnông Bu Nor</b>: in <a href="/wiki/%C4%90%E1%BA%AFk_N%C3%B4ng_Province" class="mw-redirect" title="Đắk Nông Province">Đắk Nông District</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=%C4%90%E1%BA%AFk_Min_District&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Đắk Min District (page does not exist)">Đắk Min District</a></li>
<li><b>Mnông Dih Bri</b>: very small population in <a href="/wiki/%C4%90%E1%BA%AFk_N%C3%B4ng_Province" class="mw-redirect" title="Đắk Nông Province">Đắk Nông District</a>; Êa Krông.</li>
<li><b>Mnông Đíp</b>: <a href="/w/index.php?title=%C4%90%E1%BA%AFk_Min_District&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Đắk Min District (page does not exist)">Đắk Min District</a> and the northern part of former <a href="/wiki/S%C3%B4ng_B%C3%A9_Province" class="mw-redirect" title="Sông Bé Province">Sông Bé Province</a>.</li>
<li><b>Mnông Biat</b>: small population in former <a href="/wiki/S%C3%B4ng_B%C3%A9_Province" class="mw-redirect" title="Sông Bé Province">Sông Bé Province</a>. Majority living around the Vietnam-Cambodia border.</li>
<li><b>Mnông Bu Đêh</b>: in former <a href="/wiki/S%C3%B4ng_B%C3%A9_Province" class="mw-redirect" title="Sông Bé Province">Sông Bé Province</a> and <a href="/wiki/%C4%90%E1%BA%AFk_L%E1%BA%AFk_Province" class="mw-redirect" title="Đắk Lắk Province">Đắk Lắk Province</a></li>
<li><b>Mnông Si Tô</b>: a group of <a href="/wiki/M%E1%BA%A1_people" title="Mạ people">Mạ</a> (Mạ Tô) people in <a href="/wiki/%C4%90%E1%BA%AFk_N%C3%B4ng_Province" class="mw-redirect" title="Đắk Nông Province">Đắk Nông District</a> who have become assimilated into the Mnông population ("Mnông-ized" Mạ people)</li>
<li><b>Mnông K’ah</b>: a group of <a href="/wiki/Rade_people" title="Rade people">Ê-đê</a> people scattered across <a href="/wiki/%C4%90%E1%BA%AFk_N%C3%B4ng_Province" class="mw-redirect" title="Đắk Nông Province">Đắk Nông District</a>, <a href="/wiki/L%E1%BA%AFk_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Lắk District">Lắk District</a>, and <a href="/wiki/M%27%C4%90r%C4%83k_District" class="mw-redirect" title="M&#39;Đrăk District">M'Đrăk District</a> who have become assimilated into the Mnông population ("Mnông-ized" Ê-đê people)</li>
<li><b>Mnông Phê Đâm</b>: small population living only in Quảng Tín <a href="/wiki/Communes_of_vietnam" class="mw-redirect" title="Communes of vietnam">commune</a>, <a href="/wiki/%C4%90%E1%BA%AFk_N%C3%B4ng_Province" class="mw-redirect" title="Đắk Nông Province">Đắk Nông District</a>.</li></ul>
<p>Other minor Mnong <a href="/wiki/Ethnic_group" class="mw-redirect" title="Ethnic group">ethnic groups</a> include the <b>Mnông Rơ Đe</b>, <b>Mnông R’Ông</b>, and <b>Mnông K’Ziêng</b>.
</p><p>Nguyễn &amp; Trương (2009) cover the following M'Nông dialects.
</p>
<ul><li>M'Nông Preh</li>
<li>Kuênh</li>
<li>Mạ</li>
<li>M'Nông Nâr (Bu Nâr)</li>
<li>M'Nông Noong (Bu Noong)</li>
<li>M'Nông R'Lâm</li>
<li>M'Nông Prâng</li></ul>
