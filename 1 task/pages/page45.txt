<p>The <b>Even language</b> <span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt" lang="en-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ə/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;about&#39;">ə</span><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="&#39;v&#39; in &#39;vie&#39;">v</span><span title="/ɛ/: &#39;e&#39; in &#39;dress&#39;">ɛ</span><span title="&#39;n&#39; in &#39;nigh&#39;">n</span></span>/</a></span></span> <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i title="English pronunciation respelling">ə-<span style="font-size:90%">VEN</span></i></a>, also known as <b>Lamut</b>, <b>Ewen</b>, <b>Eben</b>, <b>Orich</b>, <b>Ilqan</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Эве́нский язы́к</span>, <small><a href="/wiki/Romanization_of_Russian" title="Romanization of Russian">romanized</a>:&#160;</small><span title="Russian-language romanization"><i lang="ru-Latn">Evénsky yazýk</i></span>, historically also <span title="Russian-language text"><span lang="ru">Ламутский язы́к</span></span>, <span title="Russian-language romanization"><i lang="ru-Latn">Lamutsky yazýk</i></span>), is a <a href="/wiki/Tungusic_languages" title="Tungusic languages">Tungusic</a> language spoken by the <a href="/wiki/Evens" title="Evens">Evens</a> in <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a>. It is spoken by widely scattered communities of <a href="/wiki/Reindeer_herding" title="Reindeer herding">reindeer herders</a> from <a href="/wiki/Kamchatka" class="mw-redirect" title="Kamchatka">Kamchatka</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Okhotsk" title="Sea of Okhotsk">Sea of Okhotsk</a> in the east to the <a href="/wiki/Lena_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Lena River">Lena</a> river in the west and from the <a href="/wiki/Arctic_coast" class="mw-redirect" title="Arctic coast">Arctic coast</a> in the north to the <a href="/wiki/Aldan_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Aldan River">Aldan</a> river in the south. Even is an <a href="/wiki/Endangered_language" title="Endangered language">endangered language</a> with only some 5,700 speakers (Russian census, 2010). These speakers are specifically from the <a href="/wiki/Magadan" title="Magadan">Magadan</a> region, the <a href="/wiki/Chukotka_Autonomous_Okrug" title="Chukotka Autonomous Okrug">Chukot</a> region and the <a href="/wiki/Koryaks" title="Koryaks">Koryak</a> region.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The dialects are Arman, Indigirka, Kamchatka, Kolyma-Omolon, Okhotsk, Ola, Tompon, Upper Kolyma, Sakkyryr and Lamunkhin.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the regions where the Evens primarily reside, the Even language is generally taught in pre-school and elementary school alongside the national language, <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>. Where Even functioned primarily as an <a href="/wiki/Oral_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Oral language">oral language</a> for communication between reindeer herding brigades, textbooks began circulating throughout these educational institutions from around 1925 to 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-lingsib.iea.ras.ru_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lingsib.iea.ras.ru-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The syntax of the Even language follows the <a href="/wiki/Nominative_case" title="Nominative case">nominative case</a> and subject-object-verb (SOV) word order, with the attribute preceding the dependent member.<sup id="cite_ref-lingsib.iea.ras.ru_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lingsib.iea.ras.ru-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Language_contact">Language contact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Even_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Language contact"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In some remote Arctic villages, such as <a href="/wiki/Russkoye_Ustye" title="Russkoye Ustye">Russkoye Ustye</a>, whose population descended from Russian-Even intermarriage, the language spoken into the 20th century was a dialect of Russian with a strong Even influence.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Morphology_and_syntax">Morphology and syntax</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Even_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Morphology and syntax"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Even parts of speech include <a href="/wiki/Postposition" class="mw-redirect" title="Postposition">postpositions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Conjunction_(grammar)" title="Conjunction (grammar)">conjunction</a>, <a href="/wiki/Particle_(grammar)" class="mw-redirect" title="Particle (grammar)">particles</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Adverb" title="Adverb">adverbs</a>, as well as nouns and verbs; nouns in Even can function as adjectives and adverbs. Even features a <a href="/wiki/Nominative-accusative_alignment" class="mw-redirect" title="Nominative-accusative alignment">nominative-accusative alignment</a> with <a href="/wiki/Subject-object-verb_word_order" class="mw-redirect" title="Subject-object-verb word order">subject-object-verb word order</a>. There exists an obligatory <a href="/wiki/Copula_(linguistics)" title="Copula (linguistics)">copula</a>, but it can be omitted if a noun in the <a href="/wiki/Predicate_(grammar)" title="Predicate (grammar)">predicate</a> is inflected for the third person.
</p><p>Nouns in Even are marked for 13 <a href="/wiki/Grammatical_case" title="Grammatical case">cases</a>, including the <a href="/wiki/Nominative" class="mw-redirect" title="Nominative">nominative</a>, <a href="/wiki/Accusative" class="mw-redirect" title="Accusative">accusative</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dative" class="mw-redirect" title="Dative">dative</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lative" class="mw-redirect" title="Lative">lative</a>, two forms of the <a href="/wiki/Locative" class="mw-redirect" title="Locative">locative</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prolative" class="mw-redirect" title="Prolative">prolative</a>, three forms of the <a href="/wiki/Ablative" class="mw-redirect" title="Ablative">ablative</a>, <a href="/wiki/Instrumental_case" title="Instrumental case">instrumental</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Comitative" class="mw-redirect" title="Comitative">comitative</a>. They are also inflected for the singular or plural number and for possession, as well as for the subjective, which indicates that the subject noun has no object. Noun inflection is exclusively suffixing. Its pronouns are distinguished between personal, <a href="/wiki/Reflexive_pronoun" title="Reflexive pronoun">reflexive</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Possession_(linguistics)" title="Possession (linguistics)">possessive</a> forms, with a distinction between <a href="/wiki/Inalienable_possession" title="Inalienable possession">alienable and inalienable forms</a>.
</p><p>Verbs can be conjugated with prefixes for 15 <a href="/wiki/Grammatical_aspect" title="Grammatical aspect">aspects</a> and feature 6 distinctions in <a href="/wiki/Voice_(grammar)" title="Voice (grammar)">voice</a>, with specific negative and interrogative forms. There are 14 ways to form <a href="/wiki/Participle" title="Participle">participles</a>, 8 being <a href="/wiki/Transgressive_(linguistics)" title="Transgressive (linguistics)">transgressives</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Orthography">Orthography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Even_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Orthography"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Even_alphabets" title="Even alphabets">Even alphabets</a></div>
<p>At present, Even writing functions in Cyrillic. There are 3 stages in the history of Even writing:
</p>
<ul><li>until the early 1930s, early attempts to create a written language based on the Cyrillic alphabet;</li>
<li>1931-1937 - writing on the Latin basis;</li>
<li>since 1937 - modern writing based on the Cyrillic alphabet.</li></ul>
