<p>The <b>Sucite language</b> or <b>Sicite</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Senufo_languages" title="Senufo languages">Senufo</a> language spoken in southwestern <a href="/wiki/Burkina_Faso" title="Burkina Faso">Burkina Faso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mali" title="Mali">Mali</a> by approximately 35,000 people. Sucite is a close neighbour of the <a href="/wiki/Supyire_language" title="Supyire language">Supyire language</a>, spoken in southeastern Mali. Sucite is sometimes regarded as the northern extension of the Supyire language. The two dialects are, according to Garber (1987), ‘quite mutually intelligible’. Sometimes speakers of Sucite will even refer to themselves as speaking Supyire. Another closely related lect is <a href="/wiki/Mamara_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Mamara language">Mamara</a> (also known as Minyanka).
</p><p>Some other Senufo groups refers to the Sùcìté people as Tagba, because they live on the Tagouara plateau. There are various ways to spell the dialect names. Variants of Sucite include Sicite, Sipiite, and Sicire. The <a href="/wiki/SIL_International" title="SIL International">SIL</a> language code is SEP.
</p>

<p>Geminate voiced stops/affricates are cognate to prenasalized voiceless stops in <a href="/wiki/Supyire_language" title="Supyire language">Supyire</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and are indicated orthographically as <span class="nowrap">&#x27e8;mp, nt, ...&#x27e9;</span> in Garber (1987).
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tone">Tone</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sucite_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Tone"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sucite is a <a href="/wiki/Tonal_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Tonal language">tonal language</a> with three surface tone levels: High, Mid, and Low. Garber (1987) and Carlson (1994) analyse the Northern Senufo system as having two different Mid tones, a strong mid (Ms) and a weak mid (Mw). The Ms tone undergoes substantially less tonal alternations than the Mw tone. Garber (1988) suggests that this peculiarity may have its origin in a <a href="/wiki/Tone_(linguistics)#tonal_split" title="Tone (linguistics)">tonal split</a>.
</p><p>Glides formed by combining pairs of tones exist, the most common being HL and ML.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Grammar">Grammar</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sucite_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Grammar"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nouns">Nouns</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sucite_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Nouns"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Like the other Senufo languages, Sucite employs a <a href="/wiki/Noun_class" title="Noun class">noun class</a> system of five genders: three pairings of singular/plural classes and two mass/collective classes.
