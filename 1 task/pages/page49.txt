<p><b>Harzandi</b> or <b>Harzani</b> (<a href="/wiki/Tati_language_(Iran)" title="Tati language (Iran)">Tati</a>: <span title="Persian-language text"><span lang="fa" dir="rtl">هرزندی، هرزنی</span></span>) is a dialect of the <a href="/wiki/Tati_language_(Iran)" title="Tati language (Iran)">Tati language</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-henning_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-henning-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> spoken in the northern regions of the <a href="/wiki/East_Azarbaijan" class="mw-redirect" title="East Azarbaijan">East Azarbaijan</a> province of <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>. It is strictly an oral language, and a descendant of the <a href="/wiki/Old_Azeri_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Old Azeri language">Old Azeri language</a> that has long been extinct as a result of the diffusion of <a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a> in the area.<sup id="cite_ref-karimzadeh_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-karimzadeh-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="General_Information">General Information</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harzandi_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: General Information"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Harzani Tati is considered an endangered language with a little less than 30,000 speakers in present day.<sup id="cite_ref-ethnologue_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ethnologue-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Its speakers principally reside in the <a href="/wiki/Harzandat-e_Gharbi_Rural_District" title="Harzandat-e Gharbi Rural District">rural district of Harzand</a>, particularly in the village known as <a href="/wiki/Galin_Qayah" title="Galin Qayah">Galin Qayah</a>. Harzani is also present in the neighboring villages of <a href="/wiki/Babereh-ye_Olya" title="Babereh-ye Olya">Babratein</a> and <a href="/wiki/Harzand-e_Jadid" title="Harzand-e Jadid">Dash Harzand</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-karimzadeh_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-karimzadeh-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of now, Harzani has not been formally recognized by the Islamic Republic of Iran, and thus receives no government support.<sup id="cite_ref-llow_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-llow-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Language_Features">Language Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harzandi_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Language Features"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Source:<sup id="cite_ref-llow_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-llow-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Like other languages and dialects of the Iranian language family, Harzani follows a <a href="/wiki/Subject%E2%80%93object%E2%80%93verb" class="mw-redirect" title="Subject–object–verb">subject–object–verb</a> (SOV) word order. It has nine vowels, and shares a consonant inventory with <a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>. It further exhibits a <a href="/wiki/Split-ergative" class="mw-redirect" title="Split-ergative">split-ergative</a> case system: its present tense is structured to follow <a href="/wiki/Nominative-accusative_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Nominative-accusative language">nominative-accusative</a> patterning, while its past tense follows <a href="/wiki/Ergative-absolutive" class="mw-redirect" title="Ergative-absolutive">ergative-absolutive</a>.
</p><p>One characteristic that distinguishes Harzani from related <a href="/wiki/Northwestern_Iranian_languages" class="mw-redirect" title="Northwestern Iranian languages">Northwestern Iranian languages</a> is its change from an intervocalic /d/ to an /r/.<sup id="cite_ref-henning_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-henning-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It also has a tendency to lengthen its vowels. For instance, it has the closed vowel /oe/.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nouns_and_Pronouns">Nouns and Pronouns</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harzandi_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Nouns and Pronouns"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Nouns and pronouns in Harzani do not reflect <a href="/wiki/Grammatical_gender" title="Grammatical gender">grammatical gender</a>, but they do express <a href="/wiki/Grammatical_case" title="Grammatical case">case</a>. Nouns, in particular, encode two cases: <a href="/wiki/Direct_case" title="Direct case">direct</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oblique_case" title="Oblique case">oblique case</a>, the first of which is not rendered morphologically, but the second is by attaching a suffix. Meanwhile, personal pronouns have three cases: direct, oblique, and <a href="/wiki/Possessive" title="Possessive">possessive</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Verbs">Verbs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harzandi_dialect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Verbs"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Verbs in Harzani are inflected for present tense and past tense. Information concerning person and number is reflected in suffixes that attach to these two verb stems. <a href="/wiki/Linguistic_modality" class="mw-redirect" title="Linguistic modality">Modal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grammatical_aspect" title="Grammatical aspect">aspectual</a> information is expressed using prefixes.
</p>
