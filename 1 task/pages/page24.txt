<p><b>Talysh</b> (<a class="mw-selflink selflink">Talysh</a>: <span lang="tly">تؤلشه زوؤن, Tolışə Zıvon, Tолышә зывон</span>)<sup id="cite_ref-pirejko_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pirejko-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Northwestern_Iranian_languages" class="mw-redirect" title="Northwestern Iranian languages">Northwestern Iranian language</a> spoken in the northern regions of the <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iranian</a> provinces of <a href="/wiki/Gilan_Province" class="mw-redirect" title="Gilan Province">Gilan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ardabil_Province" class="mw-redirect" title="Ardabil Province">Ardabil</a> and the southern regions of the Republic of <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a> by around 500,000-800,000 people. Talysh language is closely related to the <a href="/wiki/Tati_language_(Iran)" title="Tati language (Iran)">Tati language</a>. It includes many dialects usually divided into three main clusters: Northern (in Azerbaijan and Iran), Central (Iran) and Southern (Iran). Talysh is partially, but not fully, intelligible with <a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>. Talysh is classified as "vulnerable" by <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Red_Book_of_Endangered_Languages" class="mw-redirect" title="Red Book of Endangered Languages">Atlas of the World's Languages in Danger</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span></h2>
<p>The origin of the name Talysh is not clear but is likely to be quite old. The name of the people appears in early Arabic sources as Al-Taylasân and in Persian as Tâlišân and Tavâliš, which are plural forms of Tâliš. Northern Talysh (in the Republic of Azerbaijan) was historically known as <a href="/wiki/Talish-i_Gushtasbi" title="Talish-i Gushtasbi">Tâlish-i Guštâsbi</a>. Talysh has always been mentioned with Gilan or <a href="/wiki/Mughan_plain" title="Mughan plain">Muqan</a>. Writing in the 1330s AD, Hamdallah Mostowfi calls the language of Gushtaspi (covering the Caspian border region between Gilan to <a href="/wiki/Shirvan" title="Shirvan">Shirvan</a>) a <a href="/wiki/Pahlavi_scripts" title="Pahlavi scripts">Pahlavi language</a> connected to the language of Gilan.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Although there are no confirmed records, the language called in Iranian linguistics as <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Azari_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Ancient Azari language">Azari</a> can be the antecedent of both Talysh and <a href="/wiki/Tati_(Iran)" class="mw-redirect" title="Tati (Iran)">Tati</a>. Miller's (1953) hypothesis that the Âzari of <a href="/wiki/Ardabil" title="Ardabil">Ardabil</a>, as appears in the quatrains of <a href="/wiki/Safi-ad-din_Ardabili" class="mw-redirect" title="Safi-ad-din Ardabili">Shaikh Safi</a>, was a form of Talysh was confirmed by Henning (1954).<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Asatrian_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Asatrian-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In western literature the people and the language are sometimes referred to as Talishi, Taleshi or Tolashi. Generally speaking, written documents about Taleshi are rare.
</p><p>The first information about the Talysh language in Russian can be found in Volume X of Strachevsky's "Encyclopedic Dictionary" ("Справочный энциклопедический словарь"), published in St. Petersburg in 1848.&#160; The work says:
</p><p>"The Talysh dialect is one of the six main dialects of Persian. It is used in the Talysh khanate and is probably the homeland of that language. Due to its grammatical and lexicographic forms, this language is noticeably different from other dialects.&#160; Except for the addition of the plural suffix "un", it is peculiar and is not derived from any Pahlavi or any other language. This language puts all relative pronouns before the noun, and the pronouns themselves are original in it.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The second information about the Talysh language is provided by Ilya Berezin, a professor at Kazan University, in Russian, but not in Russian, but in French.&#160; In 1853, Berezin's book on Persian grammar was published in Kazan.&#160; In the same year, his book "Recherches sur les dialectes persans" was published in Kazan.&#160; Experts still refer to this work as the first work of Russian Iranians in the field of Iranian dialectology.&#160; He used the "Talysh" songs given in A. Khodzko's work.&#160; IN Berezin's work consists of two parts - a grammatical essay and songs from A. Khodzko's work.&#160; IN Berezin writes that he conducted his research on Iranian dialects on the basis of materials he personally collected and studied, but does not write anywhere with whom, when and in what area he collected them.&#160; In the work, Talysh words are distorted.&#160; IN Berezin writes about the quartets taken from the work of A. Khodzko:
</p><p>"Here I present to the reader a new translation of the Talysh, Gilan and Mazandaran songs and accompany them with critical notes; the Talysh texts, if not in Khodzko, were restored by me on the basis of his transcription."&#160; However, the author writes that "grammatical rules are not strictly observed in the Talysh language, as the verb's news form is usually confused almost all the time, i.e. instead of the aorist preterit, the future&#160; time in the present tense, etc.&#160; is used. "&#160; Going even further, he writes: "In the Talysh language, the verb is the most difficult, the most confusing and the most dubious part."<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span></h2>
<p>In the north of Iran, there are six cities where Talysh is spoken: <a href="/wiki/Masal" title="Masal">Masal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rezvanshahr,_Gilan" title="Rezvanshahr, Gilan">Rezvanshar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hashtpar" class="mw-redirect" title="Hashtpar">Talesh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fuman" title="Fuman">Fuman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shaft,_Iran" title="Shaft, Iran">Shaft</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Masuleh" title="Masuleh">Masuleh</a> (in these cities some people speak <a href="/wiki/Gilaki_language" title="Gilaki language">Gilaki</a> and Turkish as well). The only towns where Talysh is spoken exclusively are the townships of Masal and Masuleh. In other cities, in addition to Talysh, people speak <a href="/wiki/Gilaki_language" title="Gilaki language">Gilaki</a> and <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijani_language" title="Azerbaijani language">Azerbaijani</a>. In Azerbaijan there are eight cities where Talysh is spoken<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>: <a href="/wiki/Astara,_Azerbaijan" title="Astara, Azerbaijan">Astara</a> (98%), <a href="/wiki/Lerik,_Azerbaijan" title="Lerik, Azerbaijan">Lerik</a>  (90%), <a href="/wiki/Lankaran" title="Lankaran">Lenkoran</a>  (90%), <a href="/wiki/Masall%C4%B1" title="Masallı">Masalli</a> (36%).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (November 2013)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> 
</p><p>Talysh has been under the influence of <a href="/wiki/Gilaki_language" title="Gilaki language">Gilaki</a>, Azeri Turkic, and Persian. In the south (Taleshdula, Masal, Shanderman, and Fumanat) the Talysh and <a href="/wiki/Gilaki_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Gilaki people">Gilaks</a> live side by side; however, there is less evidence that a Talysh family replaces Gilaki with its own language. In this region, the relation is more of a contribution to each other's language. In the north of Gilan, on the other hand, Azeri Turkic has replaced Talysh in cities like <a href="/wiki/Astara,_Iran" title="Astara, Iran">Astara</a> after the migration of Turkic speakers to the region decades ago. However, the people around Lavandvil and its mountainous regions have retained Talysh. Behzad Behzadi, the author of "Azerbaijani Persian Dictionary" remarks that: "The inhabitants of Astara are Talyshis and in fifty years ago (about 1953) that I remember the elders of our family spoke in that language and the great majority of dwellers also conversed in Talyshi. In the surrounding villages, a few were familiar with Turkic".<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> From around <a href="/wiki/Lisar" title="Lisar">Lisar</a> up to <a href="/wiki/Hashtpar" class="mw-redirect" title="Hashtpar">Hashtpar</a>, Azeri and Talysh live side by side, with the latter mostly spoken in small villages. To the south of <a href="/wiki/Asalem" title="Asalem">Asalem</a>, the influence of Azeri is negligible and the tendency is towards Persian along with Talysh in cities. In the Azerbaijan republic, Talysh is less under the influence of Azeri and <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a> than Talysh in Iran is affected by Persian.<sup id="cite_ref-Abdoli_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Abdoli-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Central Talysh has been considered the purest of all Talysh dialects.<sup id="cite_ref-Asatrian_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Asatrian-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Classification_and_related_languages">Classification and related languages</span></h2>
<p>Talysh belongs to the Northwestern Iranian branch of <a href="/wiki/Indo-European_languages" title="Indo-European languages">Indo-European languages</a>. The living language most closely related to Talysh is <a href="/wiki/Tati_(Iran)" class="mw-redirect" title="Tati (Iran)">Tati</a>. The Tati group of dialects is spoken across the Talysh range in the southwest<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (November 2013)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> (Kajal and Shahrud) and south (Tarom).<sup id="cite_ref-Asatrian_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Asatrian-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> This Tatic family should not be confused with another <a href="/wiki/Tat_language_(Caucasus)" title="Tat language (Caucasus)">Tat</a> family which is more related to Persian. Talysh also shares many features and structures with <a href="/wiki/Zazaki_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Zazaki language">Zazaki</a>, now spoken in <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Caspian_languages" title="Caspian languages">Caspian languages</a> and <a href="/wiki/Semnani_language" title="Semnani language">Semnani</a> of Iran.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Dialects">Dialects</span></h2>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Caspian_languages.png" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/c/c4/Caspian_languages.png/220px-Caspian_languages.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="130" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/c/c4/Caspian_languages.png/330px-Caspian_languages.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/c/c4/Caspian_languages.png/440px-Caspian_languages.png 2x" data-file-width="1251" data-file-height="742" /></a><figcaption>Map depicting areas where Talysh dialects are spoken</figcaption></figure>
<p>The division of Talysh into three clusters is based on lexical, phonological and grammatical factors.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Northern Talysh distinguishes itself from Central and Southern Talysh not only geographically but culturally and linguistically as well. Speakers of Northern Talysh are found almost exclusively in the Republic of Azerbaijan but can also be found in the neighbouring regions of Iran, in the Province of Gilan. The varieties of Talysh spoken in the Republic of Azerbaijan are best described as speech varieties rather than dialects. Four speech varieties are generally identified on the basis of phonetic and lexical differences. These are labeled according to the four major political districts in the Talysh region: <a href="/wiki/Astara_(rayon)" class="mw-redirect" title="Astara (rayon)">Astara</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lankaran_Rayon" class="mw-redirect" title="Lankaran Rayon">Lankaran</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lerik_Rayon" class="mw-redirect" title="Lerik Rayon">Lerik</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Masally_Rayon" class="mw-redirect" title="Masally Rayon">Masalli</a>. The differences between the varieties are minimal at the phonetic <sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and lexical level.<sup id="cite_ref-pirejko_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pirejko-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Mamedov (1971) suggests a more useful dialectal distinction is one between the varieties spoken in the mountains and those spoken in the plains. The morphosyntax of Northern Talysh is characterized by a complicated split system which is based on the Northwest Iranian type of accusativity/ergativity dichotomy: it shows accusative features with present-stem-based transitive constructions, whereas past-stem-based constructions tend towards an ergative behavior.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In distant regions like <a href="/wiki/Lavandevil" title="Lavandevil">Lavandevil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Masuleh" title="Masuleh">Masuleh</a>, the dialects differ to such a degree that conversations begin to be difficult.<sup id="cite_ref-Abdoli_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Abdoli-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In Iran, the northern dialect is in danger of <a href="/wiki/Language_death" title="Language death">extinction</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Scripts">Scripts</span></h2>
<p>The vowel system in Talysh is more extended than in standard Persian. The prominent differences are the front vowel ü in central and northern dialects and the central vowel ə.<sup id="cite_ref-Asatrian_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Asatrian-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 1929, a Latin-based alphabet was created for Talysh in the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>. However, in 1938 it was changed to <a href="/wiki/Cyrillic_alphabet_variants" class="mw-redirect" title="Cyrillic alphabet variants">Cyrillic-based</a>, but it did not gain extensive usage for a variety of reasons. An orthography based on <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijani_alphabet" title="Azerbaijani alphabet">Azeri Latin</a> is used in Azerbaijan,<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and also in Iranian sources, for example on the <a href="/wiki/IRIB" class="mw-redirect" title="IRIB">IRIB</a>'s ParsToday website.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Perso-Arabic_script" class="mw-redirect" title="Perso-Arabic script">Perso-Arabic script</a> is also used in Iran, although publications in the language are rare and are mostly volumes of poetry.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The following tables contain the vowels and consonants used in Talysh. The sounds of the letters on every row, pronounced in each language, may not correspond fully.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Grammar">Grammar</span></h2>
<p>Talysh has a <a href="/wiki/Subject%E2%80%93object%E2%80%93verb" class="mw-redirect" title="Subject–object–verb">subject–object–verb</a> word order. In some situations the case marker, 'i' or 'e' attaches to the accusative noun phrase. There is no definite article, and the indefinite one is "i". The plural is marked by the suffixes "un", "ēn" and also "yēn" for nouns ending with vowels. In contrast to Persian, modifiers are preceded by nouns, for example: "maryami kitav" (Mary's book) and "kava daryâ" (livid sea). Like most other Iranian dialects there are two categories of inflexion, subject and object cases. The "present stem" is used for the imperfect and the "past stem" for the present in the verbal system. That differentiates Talysh from most other Western Iranian dialects. In the present tense, verbal affixes cause a rearranging of the elements of conjugation in some dialects like Tâlešdulâbi, e.g. for expressing the negation of b-a-dašt-im (I sew), "ni" is used in the following form: ni-m-a-dašt (I don't sew)."m" is first person singular marker, "a" denotes duration and "dašt" is the past stem.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pronouns">Pronouns</span></h3>
<p>Talysh is a <a href="/wiki/Null-subject_language" title="Null-subject language">null-subject language</a>, so nominal pronouns (e.g. I, he, she) are optional. For <a href="/wiki/Grammatical_person" title="Grammatical person">first person</a> <a href="/wiki/Grammatical_number" title="Grammatical number">singular</a>, both "az" and "men" are used. Person suffixes are not added to stems for "men".<sup id="cite_ref-Abdoli_12-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Abdoli-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> 
Examples:
</p>
<ul><li>men xanda. (I read.), az bexun-em (Should I read ...)</li>
<li>men daxun! (Call me!), az-daxun-em (Should I call ...)</li></ul>
<p>There are three prefixes in Talysh and Tati added to normal forms making possessive pronouns. They are: "če / ča" and "eš / še".
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nouns_and_adpositions">Nouns and adpositions</span></h3>
<p>There are four "cases" in Talysh, the nominative (unmarked), the genitive, the (definite) accusative and ergative.
</p><p>The nominative case (characterized by null morpheme on nouns) encodes the subject; the predicate; the indefinite direct object in a nominative clause; definite direct object in an ergative clause;  the vowel-final main noun in a noun phrase with another noun modifying it; and, finally, the nominal element in an adpositional phrases with certain adpositions. The examples below are from Pirejko 1976<sup id="cite_ref-pirejko_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pirejko-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>    
</p>
<div style="display:none;">
<p>PRST:present stem
REFL:reflexive pronoun
</p>
</div>
<div class="interlinear" style="margin-left:4em"><div style="position:absolute;left:1em">ex:</div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">Nənə</p><p style="margin: 0px;">mother</p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">ıştə</p><p style="margin: 0px;"><abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="reflexive pronoun" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">REFL</abbr></p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">zoə</p><p style="margin: 0px;">son</p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">pe-də</p><p style="margin: 0px;">love.<abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="verbal noun" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">VN</abbr>-<abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="locative case" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">LOC</abbr></p></div><p style="display: none;">Nənə ıştə zoə pe-də</p><p style="display: none;">mother REFL son love.VN-LOC</p><p style="clear: left;">'The mother loves her son'</p><div style="clear: left; display: block;"></div>
</div>
<div class="interlinear" style="margin-left:4em"><div style="position:absolute;left:1em">ex:</div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">Əv</p><p style="margin: 0px;"><abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="third person, singular" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">3SG</abbr></p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">rəis-e</p><p style="margin: 0px;">boss-<abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="predicate/predicative" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">PRED</abbr></p></div><p style="display: none;">Əv rəis-e</p><p style="display: none;">3SG boss-PRED</p><p style="clear: left;">'S/he is a boss'</p><div style="clear: left; display: block;"></div>
</div>
<div class="interlinear" style="margin-left:4em"><div style="position:absolute;left:1em">ex:</div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">Az</p><p style="margin: 0px;"><abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="first person, singular" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">1SG</abbr></p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">vıl</p><p style="margin: 0px;">flower</p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">bı-çın-ım</p><p style="margin: 0px;"><abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="future tense" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">FUT</abbr>-pick.<abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="present stem" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">PRST</abbr>-<abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="future tense" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">FUT</abbr></p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">bo</p><p style="margin: 0px;">for</p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">tını</p><p style="margin: 0px;"><abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="second person, singular" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">2SG</abbr>.<abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="ergative case" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">ERG</abbr></p></div><p style="display: none;">Az vıl bı-çın-ım bo tını</p><p style="display: none;">1SG flower FUT-pick.PRST-FUT for 2SG.ERG</p><p style="clear: left;">'I will pick a flower for you'</p><div style="clear: left; display: block;"></div>
</div>
<div class="interlinear" style="margin-left:4em"><div style="position:absolute;left:1em">ex:</div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">Əy</p><p style="margin: 0px;"><abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="third person, singular" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">3SG</abbr>.<abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="ergative case" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">ERG</abbr></p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">çımı</p><p style="margin: 0px;"><abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="first person, singular" style="font-variant: small-caps; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">dəftər</p><p style="margin: 0px;">notebook</p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">dıry-əşe</p><p style="margin: 0px;">tear.apart.<abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="past/passive participle" style="font-variant: small-caps; display: block;"></div>
</div>
<div class="interlinear" style="margin-left:4em"><div style="position:absolute;left:1em">ex:</div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">hovə</p><p style="margin: 0px;">sister</p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">şol</p><p style="margin: 0px;">scarf</p></div><p style="display: none;">hovə şol</p><p style="display: none;">sister scarf</p><p style="clear: left;">'sister's scarf'</p><div style="clear: left; display: block;"></div>
</div>
<div class="interlinear" style="margin-left:4em"><div style="position:absolute;left:1em">ex:</div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">bə</p><p style="margin: 0px;">to</p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">şəhr</p><p style="margin: 0px;">city</p></div><p style="display: none;">bə şəhr</p><p style="display: none;">to city</p><p style="clear: left;">'to the city'</p><div style="clear: left; display: block;"></div>
</div>
<p>The ergative case, on the other hand, has the following functions: indicating the subject of an ergative phrase; definite direct object (in this function, ergative case takes the form of <b>-ni</b> after vowel-final stems); nominal modifier in a noun phrase; the nominal element in adpositional phrases with most adpositions.
</p>
<div class="interlinear" style="margin-left:4em"><div style="position:absolute;left:1em">ex:</div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">Ağıl-<b>i</b></p><p style="margin: 0px;">child-<abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="ergative case" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">ERG</abbr></p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">sef</p><p style="margin: 0px;">apple</p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">şo</p><p style="margin: 0px;">?</p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">do-şe</p><p style="margin: 0px;">throw.<abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="past/passive participle" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">PP</abbr>-<abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="third person, singular" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">3SG</abbr>.<abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="perfective aspect" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">PFV</abbr>.<abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="ergative case" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">ERG</abbr></p></div><p style="display: none;">Ağıl-<b>i</b> sef şo do-şe</p><p style="display: none;">child-ERG apple&#160;? throw.PP-3SG.PFV.ERG</p><p style="clear: left;">'the child threw the apple'</p><div style="clear: left; display: block;"></div>
</div>
<div class="interlinear" style="margin-left:4em"><div style="position:absolute;left:1em">ex:</div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">Im</p><p style="margin: 0px;"><abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="demonstrative" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">DEM</abbr></p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">kəpot-<b>i</b></p><p style="margin: 0px;">dress-<abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="ergative case" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">ERG</abbr></p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">se-də-m</p><p style="margin: 0px;">buy.<abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="verbal noun" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">VN</abbr>-<abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="locative case" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">LOC</abbr>-<abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="first person, singular" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">1SG</abbr></p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">bə</p><p style="margin: 0px;">for</p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">həvə-yo</p><p style="margin: 0px;">sister-<abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="benefactive case (&#39;for&#39;)" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">BEN</abbr></p></div><p style="display: none;">Im kəpot-<b>i</b> se-də-m bə həvə-yo</p><p style="display: none;">DEM dress-ERG buy.VN-LOC-1SG for sister-BEN</p><p style="clear: left;">'I'm buying this dress for (my) sister'</p><div style="clear: left; display: block;"></div>
</div>
<div class="interlinear" style="margin-left:4em"><div style="position:absolute;left:1em">ex:</div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">Iştə</p><p style="margin: 0px;"><abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="reflexive pronoun" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">REFL</abbr></p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">zoə-<b>ni</b></p><p style="margin: 0px;">son-<abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="ergative case" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">ERG</abbr></p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">voğan-də</p><p style="margin: 0px;">send.<abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="verbal noun" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">VN</abbr>-<abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="locative case" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">LOC</abbr></p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">bə</p><p style="margin: 0px;">to</p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">məktəb</p><p style="margin: 0px;">school</p></div><p style="display: none;">Iştə zoə-<b>ni</b> voğan-də bə məktəb</p><p style="display: none;">REFL son-ERG send.VN-LOC to school</p><p style="clear: left;">'S/he is sending his/her son to the school'</p><div style="clear: left; display: block;"></div>
</div>
<div class="interlinear" style="margin-left:4em"><div style="position:absolute;left:1em">ex:</div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">jen-<b>i</b></p><p style="margin: 0px;">woman-<abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="ergative case" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">ERG</abbr></p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">dəs</p><p style="margin: 0px;">hand</p></div><p style="display: none;">jen-<b>i</b> dəs</p><p style="display: none;">woman-ERG hand</p><p style="clear: left;">'a hand's woman'</p><div style="clear: left; display: block;"></div>
</div>
<div class="interlinear" style="margin-left:4em"><div style="position:absolute;left:1em">ex:</div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">muallimi-<b>i</b></p><p style="margin: 0px;">teacher-<abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="ergative case" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">ERG</abbr></p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">ton-i-ku</p><p style="margin: 0px;">side-<abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="ergative case" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">ERG</abbr>-<abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="ablative case" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">ABL</abbr></p></div><div style="float: left; margin-bottom: 0.3em;margin-right: 1em;"><p style="margin: 0px;font-style: italic;">omə-m</p><p style="margin: 0px;">come.<abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="first person, singular" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">1SG</abbr>.<abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="past/passive participle" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">PP</abbr>-<abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="perfective aspect" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">PFV</abbr>.<abbr class="gloss-abbr" title="nominative case" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-variant-numeric: oldstyle-nums; text-transform: lowercase;">NOM</abbr></p></div><p style="display: none;">muallimi-<b>i</b> ton-i-ku omə-m</p><p style="display: none;">teacher-ERG side-ERG-ABL come.1SG.PP-PFV.NOM</p><p style="clear: left;">'I approached the teacher'</p><div style="clear: left; display: block;"></div>
</div>
<p>The accusative form is often used to express the simple indirect object in addition to the direct object. These "cases" are in origin actually just particles, similar to Persian prepositions like "râ".
