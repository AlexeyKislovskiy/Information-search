<p><b>Réunion Creole</b>, or <b>Reunionese Creole</b> (<a href="/wiki/R%C3%A9union_Creole_French_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Réunion Creole French language">Réunion Creole French</a>: <i lang="rcf">kréol rénioné</i>; <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <i lang="fr">créole réunionnais</i>), is a <a href="/wiki/French-based_creole_languages" title="French-based creole languages">French-based</a> <a href="/wiki/Creole_language" title="Creole language">creole language</a> spoken on <a href="/wiki/R%C3%A9union" title="Réunion">Réunion</a>. It is derived mainly from <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> and includes terms from <a href="/wiki/Malagasy_language" title="Malagasy language">Malagasy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hindi" title="Hindi">Hindi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gujarati_language" title="Gujarati language">Gujarati</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tamil_language" title="Tamil language">Tamil</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-chaudenson_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chaudenson-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In recent years, there has been an effort to develop a spelling dictionary and grammar rules. Partly because of the lack of an official orthography but also because schools are taught in French, Réunion Creole is rarely written. Notably, two translations of the French comic <i><a href="/wiki/Asterix" title="Asterix">Asterix</a></i> have been published.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Réunion Creole is the main <a href="/wiki/Vernacular" title="Vernacular">vernacular</a> of the island and is used in most colloquial and familiar settings. It is, however, in a state of <a href="/wiki/Diglossia" title="Diglossia">diglossia</a> with French as the <a href="/wiki/Prestige_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Prestige language">high language</a> – Réunion Creole is used in informal settings and conversations, while French is the language of writing, education, administration and more formal conversations.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R%C3%A9union_Creole&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Reunionese Creole first formed within the first 50 years of Reunion being inhabited.<sup id="cite_ref-chaudenson_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chaudenson-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Most of the people living in Reunion were French, Malagasy or <a href="/wiki/Indo-Portuguese" class="mw-redirect" title="Indo-Portuguese">Indo-Portuguese</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-chaudenson_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chaudenson-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Most families at this time had at least one native French speaker.<sup id="cite_ref-holm_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-holm-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It is now the native language of 90% of the island's population.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
