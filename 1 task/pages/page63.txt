<p><b>Basaa</b> (also spelled <i>Bassa, Basa, Bissa</i>), or <b>Mbene</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Bantu_languages" title="Bantu languages">Bantu language</a> spoken in <a href="/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Bassa_people_(Cameroon)" title="Bassa people (Cameroon)">Basaa people</a>. It is spoken by about 300,000 people in the <a href="/wiki/Centre_Province,_Cameroon" class="mw-redirect" title="Centre Province, Cameroon">Centre</a> and <a href="/wiki/Littoral_Province,_Cameroon" class="mw-redirect" title="Littoral Province, Cameroon">Littoral</a> regions.
</p><p>Maho (2009) lists <a href="/wiki/Kogo_language" title="Kogo language">North and South Kogo</a> as dialects.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_Origin">Background and Origin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Basaa_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and Origin"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Basaa is spoken by 230,000 speakers. They live in <a href="/wiki/Nyong-et-Kelle" class="mw-redirect" title="Nyong-et-Kelle">Nyong-et-Kelle</a> (Central Region) and <a href="/wiki/Sanaga_Maritime" class="mw-redirect" title="Sanaga Maritime">Sanaga Maritime</a> (with the exception of the <a href="/wiki/Ed%C3%A9a" title="Edéa">Edéa</a> commune, which has a <a href="/wiki/Bakoko_people" title="Bakoko people">Bakoko</a> majority) and most of <a href="/wiki/Nkam" title="Nkam">Nkam</a> commune (<a href="/wiki/Littoral_Region_(Cameroon)" title="Littoral Region (Cameroon)">Littoral Region</a>). In the western and northern parts of this department, the peripheral Basaa dialects are spoken: Yabasi in the commune of <a href="/wiki/Yabassi" title="Yabassi">Yabassi</a>, Diɓuum in the commune of <a href="/wiki/Nkondjok" class="mw-redirect" title="Nkondjok">Nkondjok</a> (Diboum Canton), north of <a href="/wiki/Ndemli" class="mw-redirect" title="Ndemli">Ndemli</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dimbamban&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dimbamban (page does not exist)">Dimbamban</a>.
</p><p>Similarly, Basaa Baduala is spoken in <a href="/wiki/Wouri_Department" class="mw-redirect" title="Wouri Department">Wouri Department</a> (Littoral Region), traditional Basaa territory that is being transformed by the growth of <a href="/wiki/Douala" title="Douala">Douala</a>. Basaa is also found in <a href="/wiki/Oc%C3%A9an" title="Océan">Océan</a> Department (commune of <a href="/wiki/Bipindi" title="Bipindi">Bipindi</a>, Southern Region).
</p><p>Hijuk is spoken only in the quarter of Niki in <a href="/wiki/Grand_Batanga" title="Grand Batanga">Batanga</a> commune, in <a href="/wiki/Yangben" class="mw-redirect" title="Yangben">Yangben</a> Canton (Ch. Paulian (1980)) by 400 people. Hijuk is a Basaa dialect, despite its geographical location in the southeast of <a href="/wiki/Bokito,_Cameroon" title="Bokito, Cameroon">Bokito</a> arrondissement (<a href="/wiki/Mbam-et-Inoubou" title="Mbam-et-Inoubou">Mbam-et-Inoubou</a> department, Central Region).<sup id="cite_ref-ALCAM2012_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ALCAM2012-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tone">Tone</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Basaa_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Tone"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Basaa contrasts four <a href="/wiki/Tone_(linguistics)" title="Tone (linguistics)">tones</a>: high, low, high-to-low (falling) and low-to-high (rising).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Orthography">Orthography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Basaa_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Orthography"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
