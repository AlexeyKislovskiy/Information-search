<p><b>Kalto</b>, or <b>Nahali</b>, is an <a href="/wiki/Indo-Aryan_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Indo-Aryan language">Indo-Aryan language</a> of <a href="/wiki/Languages_of_India" title="Languages of India">India</a>. Kalto is the <a href="/wiki/Endonym" class="mw-redirect" title="Endonym">endonym</a>; the exonym "Nahal" or "Nihal" is disparaging. Because of the name "Nahali", the language has often been confused with <a href="/wiki/Nihali_language" title="Nihali language">Nihali</a>, an apparent <a href="/wiki/Language_isolate" title="Language isolate">language isolate</a> spoken by a neighbouring people with a similar lifestyle.
</p>
