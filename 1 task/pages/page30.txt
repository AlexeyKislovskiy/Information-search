<p>The <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish language</a> is widely spoken in most of the Catalan-speaking territories, where it is partly characterized by <a href="/wiki/Language_contact" title="Language contact">language contact</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">Catalan language</a>. These territories are: <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">Catalonia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Valencian_Community" title="Valencian Community">Valencian Community</a> (except some inland areas which are only Spanish-speaking), the <a href="/wiki/Balearic_Islands" title="Balearic Islands">Balearic Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andorra" title="Andorra">Andorra</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/La_Franja" title="La Franja">easternmost areas</a> of <a href="/wiki/Aragon" title="Aragon">Aragon</a>. This linguistic contact is encouraged by the fact that almost all of the Catalan speakers in these regions are Catalan–Spanish <a href="/wiki/Bilingualism" class="mw-redirect" title="Bilingualism">bilingual</a> to a greater or lesser extent.
</p><p>Many of the features of this <a href="/wiki/Spanish_dialects_and_varieties" title="Spanish dialects and varieties">Spanish language variety</a> are present due to the transfer of distinctive features of the <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">Catalan language</a>. Many speakers whose native language is Catalan feature an accent brought about through the transfer of phonetic and phonological features from Catalan; such features are recognized by the listener as a "Catalan accent".
</p><p>Some of the listed features can sometimes be found in native Spanish speakers who live in Catalan-speaking areas; however, in the case of speakers who are not bilingual, this happens almost exclusively with lexical features.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Linguistic_features">Linguistic features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Linguistic_features_of_Spanish_as_spoken_by_Catalan_speakers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Linguistic features"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>How a Catalan speaker's Spanish manifests depends heavily on individual sociolinguistic variables related to age, native language, and the differing environments of language use. It is therefore not a uniform variety with little variation. Many of the features listed below are present with very different frequencies in different speakers, and some of the features could be absent in many speakers (particularly those whose main native language is Spanish, who transfer fewer typical Catalan features).
</p><p>Most Spanish speakers in Catalan-speaking territories use linguistic forms that are not regionally marked; that is, their speech is similar to that of much of Spain; however, there is a tendency, especially among members of the working class, to use forms typical of southern Spanish dialects.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Phonetics">Phonetics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Linguistic_features_of_Spanish_as_spoken_by_Catalan_speakers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Phonetics"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The phonetic features listed below occur much more frequently among speakers whose main language is Catalan than they do among speakers whose main language is Spanish. All of them can be considered transfer of phonetic features from Catalan to Spanish:
</p><p><b>Consonants</b>
</p>
<ul><li>Word-final <i>-d</i> is often devoiced and fortified to <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[t]</span>: <i>autorida<span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[t]</span></i>, <i>verda<span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[t]</span></i>, <i>amista<span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[t]</span></i>, <i>Madri<span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[t]</span></i>.
<ul><li>In Valencia, <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/d/</span> in the <i>-ada</i> suffix can be elided, as in Southern Peninsular Spanish: <i>Mocadorada</i> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[mokaðoˈɾaða]<span class="wrap"> </span>→<span class="wrap"> </span>[moka.oˈɾaː]</span> '<a href="https://ca.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mocadorada" class="extiw" title="ca:Mocadorada">Mocadorada</a>'.</li></ul></li>
<li><span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/l/</span> can be <a href="/wiki/Velarization" title="Velarization">velarized</a> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[ɫ]</span>, especially in <a href="/wiki/Syllable_coda" class="mw-redirect" title="Syllable coda">coda</a> position.</li>
<li>Less presence of <i><a href="/wiki/Ye%C3%ADsmo" title="Yeísmo">yeísmo</a></i> than among native speakers, and thus distinction of <i>ll</i> (<span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/ʎ/</span>) and <i>y</i> (<span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/ʝ/</span>). However, this feature is in decline; even in Catalan <i>yeísmo</i> is starting to take hold in many <i><a href="/wiki/Comarca" title="Comarca">comarcas</a></i>: <i>Castell</i> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[kasˈtej]</span>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup></li>
<li>Higher frequency of <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/s/</span> voicing and the occurrence thereof between vowels.
<ul><li>In part of the Valencian Community, the suffix <i>-eza</i> (<i>-esa</i> in Valencian) is commonly reduced to <i>ea</i>: <i>bellea</i> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[beˈʎe.a]</span> or <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[beˈʝe.a]</span> 'beauty'.</li></ul></li>
<li>Mainly in speakers with a limited command of Spanish, <a href="/wiki/Seseo" class="mw-redirect" title="Seseo">seseo</a>, that is, the phoneme <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/θ/</span> is realized as <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[s]</span>.</li>
<li>Also for speakers with a limited command of Spanish, and very rare nowadays, the Spanish phoneme <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/x/</span> used to be realized as <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[k]</span>.</li></ul>
<p><b>Vowels</b>
</p>
<ul><li>The high vowels <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/i,<span class="wrap"> </span>u/</span> are more open than in Spanish. Unstressed <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/i,<span class="wrap"> </span>u/</span> are centralized.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
<ul><li>In Valencian and most Balearic dialects <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/i,<span class="wrap"> </span>u/</span> are further open and centralized.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li></ul></li>
<li>The articulation of some rising <a href="/wiki/Diphthong" title="Diphthong">diphthongs</a> as separate vowels in <a href="/wiki/Hiatus_(linguistics)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hiatus (linguistics)">hiatus</a> following the articulatory habits of Catalan; that is, the sequence <u>weak vowel</u> + <u>strong vowel</u> is pronounced as two vowels in two separate syllables. Examples: <i>tiene</i> as <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[tiˈene]</span> rather than <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[ˈtjene]</span>; <i>duele</i> as <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[duˈeɫe]</span> rather than <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[ˈdwele]</span>.</li>
<li>Articulation of the rising <a href="/wiki/Diphthong" title="Diphthong">diphthong</a> <i>iu</i> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[ju]</span> as falling <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[iw]</span>. Example: <i>ciudad</i> as <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[θiwˈðat]</span> rather than <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[θjuˈðað]</span>.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Morphology">Morphology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Linguistic_features_of_Spanish_as_spoken_by_Catalan_speakers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Morphology"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>Formation of <a href="/wiki/Diminutive" title="Diminutive">diminutives</a> the Catalan way, with <i>-ete</i> (<i>-et</i> in Catalan) and <i>-eta</i>. Although these forms are more common in Catalan-speaking territories, they also occur in other regions where Spanish is spoken,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Avoid_weasel_words" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Avoid weasel words"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too vague attribution or weasel words. (December 2015)">which?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> especially in eastern Spain.</li>
<li>More frequent use of the adjective nominalization suffix <i>-eza</i>, even with three-syllable adjectives that in Spanish most frequently use <i>-ez</i>. For example:  <i>esbelteza</i> instead of <i>esbeltez</i>.</li>
<li>The use of <i>ves</i> (from Catalan <i>vés</i>) as the second-person singular informal (<i>tú</i>) imperative of the verb <i>ir</i>, instead of the standard <i>ve</i>: <i>Ves a casa y tráeme la chaqueta</i> for <i>Ve a casa y tráeme el abrigo</i> ('Go home and bring me the coat').</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Syntax">Syntax</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Linguistic_features_of_Spanish_as_spoken_by_Catalan_speakers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Syntax"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The following features are common:
</p>
<ul><li>The use of the preposition <i>sin</i> like Catalan <i>sense</i>, which can be used adverbially without a complement; therefore, for example, exchanges such as the following can occur: A: ¿Traes la raqueta? B: He venido <i>sin</i> (for <i>He venido sin ella</i>)</li>
<li>The appearance of the particle <i>que</i> at the beginning of questions: <i>¿Que te gusta el piso?</i> instead of <i>¿Te gusta el piso?</i> ('Do you like the flat?')</li>
<li>The use of possessive pronouns instead of various sequences of <i>de</i> + <u>strong objective pronoun</u>: <i>Vete delante mío</i> for <i>Vete delante de mí</i>, <i>Vamos detrás suyo</i> for <i>Vamos detrás de él</i>. This phenomenon also occurs in many other varieties of Spanish<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Avoid_weasel_words" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Avoid weasel words"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too vague attribution or weasel words. (December 2015)">which?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>; this occurs because of analogy with such pairs as <i>izquierda de mí</i> and <i>izquierda mía</i><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>.</li>
<li>Tendency to use the definite article with the names of people, often considered <a href="/wiki/Slang" title="Slang">slang</a> in other Spanish-speaking areas: <i>el Jordi</i>, <i>la Elena</i>. There are Spanish-speaking regions not influenced by Catalan in which this also occurs<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Avoid_weasel_words" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Avoid weasel words"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too vague attribution or weasel words. (December 2015)">which?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>. This is different from the standard Spanish use of the definite article with personal names in such sentences as <i>la María que tú conoces es mi novia, no mi tía</i> ('the María that you know is my girlfriend, not my aunt').</li>
<li>The occasional preference of <i>haber de</i> + <span class="smallcaps"><span style="font-variant: small-caps; text-transform: lowercase;">INFINITIVE</span></span> instead of  <i>tener que</i> + <span class="smallcaps"><span style="font-variant: small-caps; text-transform: lowercase;">INFINITIVE</span></span> ('(to) have to'). Although <i>haber de</i> does exist in standard Spanish, it is far more common to use <i>tener que</i>.</li>
<li>Inflecting existential <i>haber</i> ('there be', as in "There is a cat on the porch.") such that it agrees in number with the complement
<dl><dd><i>Habían cuatro jueces en la competición</i> rather than <i>Había cuatro jueces en la competición</i> ('There were four judges in the competition')</dd></dl></li></ul>
<p>This also occurs in some non-Catalan-speaking areas<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Avoid_weasel_words" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Avoid weasel words"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too vague attribution or weasel words. (December 2015)">which?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>; it is a typical feature of native Spanish speakers who were born in areas where historically the local speech was particularly divergent from standard Spanish<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag needs further explanation. (December 2015)">further explanation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> (such as Zamora, Cáceres, Navarra, Murcia). It is virtually nonexistent in the core area of Burgos-Madrid-Andalusia.
</p>
<ul><li>The use of certain prepositions the Catalan way:
<dl><dd><i>Estoy aquí a Barcelona</i> for <i>Estoy aquí en Barcelona</i></dd></dl></li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Deque%C3%ADsmo" title="Dequeísmo">Dequeísmo</a></i>: <i>Pienso de ir al teatro</i> / <i>Considero de que debería venir tu hermano</i>. There are, however, internal reasons of Spanish grammar that cause the occurrence of this phenomenon outside this linguistic area<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Avoid_weasel_words" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Avoid weasel words"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too vague attribution or weasel words. (December 2015)">which?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>.</li>
<li>Extended use of <i>hacer</i> in periphrastic expressions: <i>hacer un café con alguien</i> for <i>tomar un café con alguien</i> ('have coffee with someone'), <i>hacer piña</i> for <i>mantenerse unidos</i> ('(to) stay united'), <i>hacer país</i> for <i>ser patriota</i> ('(to) be patriotic'), and so forth.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lexicon">Lexicon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Linguistic_features_of_Spanish_as_spoken_by_Catalan_speakers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Lexicon"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>Constructions such as <i>hacer tarde</i> (from the Catalan <i>fer tard</i>), <i>hacer un café</i> for <i>tomar un café</i>, <i>sacarme la camisa</i> for <i>quitarme la camisa</i>, <i>tampoco no</i> for <i>tampoco</i>, and  <i>plegar del trabajo</i> for <i>salir del trabajo</i>.</li>
<li>It is very common, especially in Catalonia, for the expression "Déu n'hi do!" (an exclamation of conformation, adequacy, or admiration: '¡No está nada mal!, ¡Es bastante!; literally 'God gave (enough)!' in an older form of Catalan<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>) to be used, especially since there is no exact equivalent in Spanish.</li>
<li>It is also common to use the Catalan word <i>adéu</i> instead of <i>adiós</i> ('goodbye').</li>
<li>In the Valencian Country, Spanish speakers that learn Valencian in school sometimes use Valencian expressions like <i>che</i> (written <i>xe</i> in Valencian) or "Prou!" (instead of "¡Basta!") in their Spanish. The phrase <i>no cal</i> is also used instead of <i>no hace falta</i> ('it is not necessary'), despite the fact that the Castilianism <i>no fa falta</i> finds use in Valencian.</li>
<li>Particularly in the Balearic Islands, it is very common to express objections with <i>pero</i> (pronounced as though it were <i>peró</i>, with stress on the second syllable, like Catalan <i>però</i>) at the end of the sentence, as in:
<dl><dd>"No viniste, peró!"</dd>
<dd>"Yo no he sido, peró!"</dd></dl></li>
<li>The frequent use of <i>prestache</i> (from the Catalan <i>prestatge</i> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/pɾesˈtaddʒe/</span>) to refer what is referred to as <i>estante</i> in standard Spanish, the use of <i>rachola</i> (from the Catalan <i>rajola</i> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/raˈdʒɔla/</span>) to refer what is referred to as <i>baldosa</i> or <i>azulejo</i> in standard Spanish, and the use of <i>tocho</i> (from the Catalan <i>totxo</i> <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/ˈtotʃo/</span>) to refer to a <i>ladrillo</i>: brick (figuratively, a thick book).</li>
<li>Some food terms derived from Catalan can be found in Spanish-language menus in Catalonia: <i>barat</i> (Catalan <i>verat</i>) for <i>caballa</i> (mackerel), <i>monchetas</i> (Catalan <i>mongetes</i>) for <i>judías</i> (beans), <i>toñina</i> (Catalan <i>tonyina</i>) for <i>atún</i> (tuna).</li>
<li>Other examples are <i>enchegar</i> (from the Catalan <i>engegar</i>) instead of <i>encender</i> or <i>prender</i>, or <i>nen</i> instead of <i>niño</i>.</li></ul>
