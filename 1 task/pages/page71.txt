<p><b>Abanyom</b>, or <b>Bakor</b>, is a language of the <a href="/wiki/Ekoid_languages" title="Ekoid languages">Ekoid</a> subfamily of <a href="/wiki/Niger%E2%80%93Congo_languages" title="Niger–Congo languages">Niger–Congo</a>.   It is spoken by the Abanyom people in the <a href="/wiki/Cross_River_State" title="Cross River State">Cross River State</a> region of <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigeria</a>.  A member of the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Bantoid_languages" title="Southern Bantoid languages">Southern Bantoid</a> group, Abanyom is fairly closely related to the <a href="/wiki/Bantu_languages" title="Bantu languages">Bantu languages</a>. It is <a href="/wiki/Tone_(linguistics)" title="Tone (linguistics)">tonal</a> and has a typical Niger–Congo <a href="/wiki/Noun_class" title="Noun class">noun class</a> system.
</p><p>Abanyom is also a clan/Ward in Ikom. It comprises the following Communities; Edor, Abangork, Akumabal, Abinti, Nkim, Nkum, Nkarassi 11, Nkarassi 1, Abankang, Etikpe, and Nkonfap. 
Abankang is referred to as the mother of Abanyom.
</p>
