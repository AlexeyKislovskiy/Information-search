<p><b>Tepehuán</b> (Tepehuano) is the name of three closely related languages of the <a href="/wiki/Piman_languages" title="Piman languages">Piman</a> branch of the <a href="/wiki/Uto-Aztecan_languages" title="Uto-Aztecan languages">Uto-Aztecan</a> language family, all spoken in northern <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>.  The language is called <b>O'otham</b> by its speakers.
</p>

<h2><span id="Northern_Tepehu.C3.A1n"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Northern_Tepehuán">Northern Tepehuán</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tepehu%C3%A1n_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Northern Tepehuán"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Northern Tepehuán is spoken by about 10,000 people (2020 census)<sup id="cite_ref-inegi2020_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inegi2020-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> in several settlements in <a href="/wiki/Guadalupe_y_Calvo_Municipality" title="Guadalupe y Calvo Municipality">Guadalupe y Calvo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guachochi" title="Guachochi">Guachochi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chihuahua_(state)" title="Chihuahua (state)">Chihuahua</a>, as well as in the north of <a href="/wiki/Durango" title="Durango">Durango</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="Southern_Tepehu.C3.A1n"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Southern_Tepehuán">Southern Tepehuán</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tepehu%C3%A1n_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Southern Tepehuán"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Southern Tepehuán is spoken by about 45,000 people,<sup id="cite_ref-inegi2020_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inegi2020-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> about equally divided into:
</p>
<ul><li>Southeastern Tepehuán in  Mezquital Municipio in the state of <a href="/wiki/Durango" title="Durango">Durango</a>.</li>
<li>Southwestern Tepehuán in southwestern Durango.</li></ul>
<p>Southern Tepehuán coexists with the <a href="/wiki/Mexicanero_language" title="Mexicanero language">Mexicanero language</a>; there is some intermarriage between the two ethnic groups, and a number of speakers are trilingual in Mexicanero, Tepehuán and <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media">Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tepehu%C3%A1n_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Media"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tepehuán-language programming is carried by the <a href="/wiki/National_Commission_for_the_Development_of_Indigenous_Peoples" class="mw-redirect" title="National Commission for the Development of Indigenous Peoples">CDI</a>'s radio stations <a href="/wiki/XEJMN-AM" title="XEJMN-AM">XEJMN-AM</a>, broadcasting from <a href="/wiki/Jes%C3%BAs_Mar%C3%ADa,_Nayarit" title="Jesús María, Nayarit">Jesús María, Nayarit</a>, and <a href="/wiki/XETAR" class="mw-redirect" title="XETAR">XETAR</a>, based in <a href="/wiki/Guachochi,_Chihuahua" class="mw-redirect" title="Guachochi, Chihuahua">Guachochi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chihuahua_(state)" title="Chihuahua (state)">Chihuahua</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Morphology">Morphology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tepehu%C3%A1n_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Morphology"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tepehuán is an <a href="/wiki/Agglutinative" class="mw-redirect" title="Agglutinative">agglutinative</a> language, in which words use suffix complexes for a variety of purposes with several <a href="/wiki/Morpheme" title="Morpheme">morphemes</a> strung together.
</p>
