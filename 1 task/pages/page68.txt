<p><b>Khalaj</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Turkic_languages" title="Turkic languages">Turkic</a> language spoken in <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>. Although it contains many old Turkic elements, it has become widely <a href="/wiki/Persianization" title="Persianization">Persianized</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKnüppel2009_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKnüppel2009-7">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEÖlmez1995_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEÖlmez1995-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>  Khalaj has about 150 words of uncertain origin.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEDoerfer1977_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDoerfer1977-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 32">&#58;&#8202;32&#8202;</span></sup>
</p><p>Surveys have found that most young <a href="/wiki/Khalaj_people" title="Khalaj people">Khalaj</a> parents do not pass the language on to their children; only 5% of families teach their children the language.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Khalaj language is a descendant of an old Turkic language called Arghu.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEJohansonCsató199881_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEJohansonCsató199881-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERobbeets20158_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERobbeets20158-9">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The 11th-century Turkic <a href="/wiki/Lexicographer" class="mw-redirect" title="Lexicographer">lexicographer</a> <a href="/wiki/Mahmud_al-Kashgari" title="Mahmud al-Kashgari">Mahmud al-Kashgari</a> was the first person to give written examples of the Khalaj language, which are mostly interchangeable with modern Khalaj.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEÖlmez1995_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEÖlmez1995-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Doerfer" title="Gerhard Doerfer">Gerhard Doerfer</a>, who rediscovered Khalaj, demonstrated that it was an independent branch from <a href="/wiki/Common_Turkic" class="mw-redirect" title="Common Turkic">Common Turkic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERobbeets20158_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERobbeets20158-9">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Classification">Classification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Khalaj_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Classification"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Turkic languages are a <a href="/wiki/Language_family" title="Language family">language family</a> of at least 35 documented languages spoken by the <a href="/wiki/Turkic_peoples" title="Turkic peoples">Turkic peoples</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEDybo2006766_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDybo2006766-10">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>While initially thought to be closely related to <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijani_language" title="Azerbaijani language">Azerbaijani</a>, linguistic studies, particularly those done by <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Doerfer" title="Gerhard Doerfer">Gerhard Doerfer</a>, led to the reclassification of Khalaj as a distinct non-<a href="/wiki/Oghuz_languages" title="Oghuz languages">Oghuz</a> branch of the Turkic language family.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKıral200089_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKıral200089-11">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Evidence for the reassignment includes the preservation of the vowel length contrasts found in <a href="/wiki/Proto-Turkic" class="mw-redirect" title="Proto-Turkic">Proto-Turkic</a> (PT),<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECheungAydemir201580_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECheungAydemir201580-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> word-initial *<i>h</i>, and the lack of the sound change *<i>d</i> → <i>y</i> characteristic of Oghuz languages.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEDoerfer1977_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDoerfer1977-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 22">&#58;&#8202;22&#8202;</span></sup>
</p><p>The conservative character of Khalaj can be seen by comparing the same words across different Turkic varieties. For example, in Khalaj, the word for "foot" is <i>hadaq</i>, while the <a href="/wiki/Cognate" title="Cognate">cognate word</a> in nearby Oghuz languages is <i>ayaq</i> (compare <a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a> <i>ayak</i>). Because of the preservation of these archaic features, some scholars have speculated that the Khalaj people are the descendants of the Arghu Turks.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKuribayashi2021469_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKuribayashi2021469-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i><a href="/wiki/Ethnologue" title="Ethnologue">Ethnologue</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/ISO_639" title="ISO 639">ISO</a> formerly listed a <a href="/wiki/Western_Iranian_languages" title="Western Iranian languages">Northwestern Iranian language</a> named "Khalaj" with the same population figure as the Turkic language.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The Khalaj speak their Turkic language and <a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>, and the supposed Iranian language of the Khalaj is <a href="/wiki/Spurious_languages" title="Spurious languages">spurious</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geographical_distribution">Geographical distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Khalaj_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geographical distribution"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/List_of_endangered_languages_in_Asia#Iran" title="List of endangered languages in Asia">List of endangered languages in Asia §&#160;Iran</a></div>
<p>Khalaj is spoken mainly in <a href="/wiki/Markazi_Province" class="mw-redirect" title="Markazi Province">Markazi Province</a> in Iran distributed throughout a number of villages from <a href="/wiki/Qom" title="Qom">Qom</a> to <a href="/wiki/Ashtian" title="Ashtian">Ashtian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tafresh" title="Tafresh">Tafresh</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Ragagnin_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ragagnin-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Doerfer cites the number of speakers as approximately 17,000 in 1968, and 20,000 in 1978.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEDoerfer1977_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDoerfer1977-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 17">&#58;&#8202;17&#8202;</span></sup> <i>Ethnologue</i> reports that the population of speakers grew to 42,107 by 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag needs to be fact-checked with the cited source(s). (March 2020)">verification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dialects">Dialects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Khalaj_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Dialects"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The main dialects of Khalaj are Northern and Southern. Within the dialect groupings, individual villages and groupings of speakers have distinct speech patterns.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>The linguistic difference between the most distant dialects is not smaller (or even bigger) than <a href="/wiki/Tatar_language" title="Tatar language">Kazan Tatar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bashkir_language" title="Bashkir language">Bashkir</a> or between <a href="/wiki/Rumelian_Turkish" class="mw-redirect" title="Rumelian Turkish">Rumelian Turkish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijani_language" title="Azerbaijani language">Azerbaijani</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEDoerfer1977_5-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDoerfer1977-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 17">&#58;&#8202;17&#8202;</span></sup>
</p>

<p>Doerfer claims that Khalaj retains three vowel lengths postulated for Proto-Turkic: long (e.g. <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[q<b>aː</b>n]</span> 'blood'), half-long (e.g. <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[b<b>aˑ</b>ʃ]</span> 'head'), and short (e.g. <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[h<b>a</b>t]</span> 'horse').<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEDoerfer1971_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDoerfer1971-19">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEDoerferTezcan1980_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDoerferTezcan1980-20">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> However, <a href="/wiki/Alexis_Manaster_Ramer" title="Alexis Manaster Ramer">Alexis Manaster Ramer</a> challenges both the interpretation that Khalaj features three vowel lengths and that Proto-Turkic had the same three-way contrast.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEManaster_Ramer1995187–88_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEManaster_Ramer1995187–88-21">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Some vowels of Proto-Turkic are realized as falling <a href="/wiki/Diphthong" title="Diphthong">diphthongs</a>, as in <span class="IPA nowrap" lang="und-Latn-fonipa" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[quo̯l]</span> ('arm').<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2022)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Morphology">Morphology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Khalaj_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Morphology"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Nouns">Nouns</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Khalaj_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Nouns"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Nouns in Khalaj might receive a <a href="/wiki/Plural" title="Plural">plural</a> marker or <a href="/wiki/Possession_(linguistics)" title="Possession (linguistics)">possessive</a> marker. <a href="/wiki/Declension" title="Declension">Cases</a> in Khalaj include <a href="/wiki/Genitive_case" title="Genitive case">genitive</a>, <a href="/wiki/Accusative_case" title="Accusative case">accusative</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dative_case" title="Dative case">dative</a>, <a href="/wiki/Locative_case" title="Locative case">locative</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ablative_case" title="Ablative case">ablative</a>, <a href="/wiki/Instrumental_case" title="Instrumental case">instrumental</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Equative_case" title="Equative case">equative</a>.
</p><p>Forms of case suffixes change based on vowel harmony and the consonants they follow. Case endings also interact with possessive suffixes. A table of basic case endings is provided below:

</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Verbs">Verbs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Khalaj_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Verbs"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Verbs in Khalaj are <a href="/wiki/Inflection" title="Inflection">inflected</a> for <a href="/wiki/Grammatical_voice" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammatical voice">voice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grammatical_tense" title="Grammatical tense">tense</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grammatical_aspect" title="Grammatical aspect">aspect</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Negation" title="Negation">negation</a>. Verbs consist of long strings of <a href="/wiki/Morpheme" title="Morpheme">morphemes</a> in the following array:
</p>
<dl><dd><b>Stem + Voice + Negation + Tense/Aspect + Agreement</b></dd></dl>
<p>Due to Persian influence, Khalaj has, like <a href="/wiki/Qashqai_language" title="Qashqai language">Qashqai</a>, lost <a href="/wiki/Converb" title="Converb">converb</a> constructions of the form <span class="nowrap">-Ib/-Ip</span>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Syntax">Syntax</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Khalaj_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Syntax"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Khalaj employs <a href="/wiki/Subject%E2%80%93object%E2%80%93verb" class="mw-redirect" title="Subject–object–verb">subject–object–verb</a> word order. <a href="/wiki/Adjective" title="Adjective">Adjectives</a> precede <a href="/wiki/Noun" title="Noun">nouns</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Vocabulary">Vocabulary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Khalaj_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Vocabulary"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The core of Khalaj vocabulary is Turkic, but many words have been borrowed from <a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>. Words from neighboring Turkic languages (namely Azerbaijani), have also made their way into Khalaj.
</p><p>For example, Khalaj numbers are Turkic in form, but some speakers replace the forms for "80" and "90" with Persian terms.
</p>
